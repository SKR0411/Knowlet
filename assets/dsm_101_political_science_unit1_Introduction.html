Politics: Meaning and Difference with Political Science and Political Theory[span_0](end_span)

Meaning of Politics

The term 'Politics' originates from the Greek word 'Polis', which means a city-state. In ancient Greece, politics was the study of everything concerning the city-state—its governance, welfare, and community life. Today, the term has a much broader and more complex meaning.

Definition: Politics is the set of activities associated with making decisions in groups, or other forms of power relations between individuals, such as the distribution of resources or status. It is often described as 'the art and science of government' or 'who gets what, when, and how'.

Politics can be viewed from two perspectives:

  1. As an Arena: This view sees politics as happening in specific places like parliaments, government offices, and international organizations. It is the formal process of governance.
  2. As a Process: This view sees politics as a widespread social activity. It is about power, conflict, and cooperation, and it can occur in any group setting—from a family to a workplace to a nation.

[span_2](start_span)[span_3](start_span)

While these terms are related, they are distinct concepts[span_2](end_span)[span_3](end_span). Understanding their differences is crucial for a strong foundation in the subject.

PoliticsThe practical activity of governing, exercising power, and resolving conflict[span_4](end_span). Practical, dynamic, and often value-laden. It's what actually happens. A political party campaigning for an election, a government passing a new law, or nations negotiating a treaty.
Political ScienceThe systematic and scientific study of politics[span_5](end_span). It seeks to describe, explain, and predict political phenomena. Academic, empirical, analytical, and objective. Political Theory: Meaning, Nature, Scope, and Relevance[span_10](end_span)

Meaning of Political Theory

[span_11](start_span)

Political Theory is a branch of political science that deals with the foundational questions and concepts of political life[span_11](end_span). It involves a critical examination of political institutions, practices, and the values that underpin them.

A political theory is an explanation of what politics is all about, a general understanding of its nature, purpose and key features. It is a framework of concepts and generalizations about political life.
[span_12](start_span)

Nature of Political Theory[span_12](end_span)

[span_13](start_span)

The nature of political theory is multifaceted and has evolved over time[span_13](end_span).

  • Normative/Prescriptive: Traditional political theory was largely normative. It sought to prescribe the 'best' or 'ideal' form of government and society (e.g., Plato's 'Ideal State'). It deals with values and moral judgments.
  • Empirical/Descriptive: Modern political theory, influenced by the behavioral revolution, emphasizes an empirical approach. It aims to describe and explain political reality as it is, based on observation and data.
  • Historical: It often involves studying the history of political ideas and thinkers to understand how our current political concepts have evolved (e.g., studying Locke to understand modern liberalism).
  • Critical: Political theory is inherently critical. It questions and challenges existing power structures, ideologies, and social norms.
[span_14](start_span)

Scope of Political Theory[span_14](end_span)

[span_15](start_span)

The scope of political theory is vast and covers a wide range of subjects[span_15](end_span):

  1. Study of the State: It examines the origin, nature, purpose, and functions of the state.
  2. Study of Government: It analyzes different forms of government (democracy, monarchy, etc.), their organs (legislature, executive, judiciary), and their processes.
  3. Study of Power and Authority: It investigates the nature of power, how it is distributed and exercised, and the basis of political authority and legitimacy.
    4. [span_16](start_span)
  4. Analysis of Core Concepts: It provides a deep analysis of fundamental political concepts such as Liberty, Equality, Justice, Rights, and Citizenship[span_16](end_span).
  5. Study of Political Ideologies: It examines major political ideologies like Liberalism, Socialism, Marxism, Feminism, and their impact on politics.
  6. Contemporary Debates: It engages with modern debates on issues like multiculturalism, globalization, environmentalism, and human rights.
[span_17](start_span)

Relevance of Political Theory[span_17](end_span)

[span_18](start_span)[span_19](start_span)

In a rapidly changing world, the relevance of political theory is more significant than ever[span_18](end_span)[span_19](end_span).

    [span_20](start_span)
  • Provides Conceptual Clarity: It helps us understand and clarify the meaning of the political concepts we use every day, like 'freedom' and 'democracy'[span_20](end_span).
  • Encourages Critical Thinking: It equips citizens to think critically about political issues, question assumptions, and evaluate the justifications for government actions.
  • Guides Political Action: By offering a vision of a just and good society, political theory can inspire and guide social and political movements aiming for reform or revolution.
  • Helps Understand Different Viewpoints: Studying different ideologies and theories helps us understand why people hold different political views and promotes tolerance and reasoned debate.
  • Foundation for Political Science: It provides the foundational concepts and normative frameworks that are essential for the empirical study of political science.
Remember This! Political theory is not just an abstract academic exercise. [span_21](start_span)[span_22](start_span)It has real-world implications[span_21](end_span)[span_22](end_span). Ideas about rights led to revolutions, and debates about justice shape public policies on taxation and welfare. Always try to connect the theory to a practical example.
