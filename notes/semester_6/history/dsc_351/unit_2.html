1. Russian Revolution 1917: Causes & Consequences

[cite_start]

The Russian Revolution was one of the most explosive political events of the 20th century, marking the end of the Romanov dynasty and the birth of the Soviet Union[cite: 699].

Causes

    [cite_start]
  • Political Oppression: The absolute and inefficient rule of Czar Nicholas II[cite: 699].
    • [cite_start]
  • Economic Distress: Widespread poverty among peasants and terrible working conditions in factories[cite: 699].
    • [cite_start]
  • Impact of WWI: Huge military losses and food shortages caused by Russia's involvement in the Great War[cite: 699].
    • [cite_start]
  • Revolutionary Ideas: The spread of Marxist ideology and the leadership of Vladimir Lenin[cite: 699].

Consequences

    [cite_start]
  • End of Autocracy: The overthrow of the monarchy and the execution of the Czar[cite: 699].
    • [cite_start]
  • Communist State: The establishment of a Bolshevik government based on communist principles[cite: 699].
    • [cite_start]
  • Civil War: A brutal internal conflict between the "Reds" (Bolsheviks) and "Whites" (anti-Bolsheviks)[cite: 699].
    • [cite_start]
  • Global Impact: Inspired socialist and communist movements worldwide[cite: 699].

2. League of Nations: Objectives & Failure

[cite_start]

Established as a result of the Paris Peace Conference, the League of Nations was the first worldwide intergovernmental organization whose principal mission was to maintain world peace[cite: 700].

Aims and Objectives

    [cite_start]
  • Collective Security: To prevent war through international cooperation[cite: 700].
    • [cite_start]
  • Disarmament: To reduce national armaments to the lowest possible point[cite: 700].
    • [cite_start]
  • Diplomatic Resolution: To settle disputes through negotiation and arbitration[cite: 700].

Organizations

    [cite_start]
  • The Assembly: Represented all member states[cite: 700].
    • [cite_start]
  • The Council: Consisted of permanent and non-permanent members[cite: 700].
    • [cite_start]
  • Secretariat: The administrative body of the League[cite: 700].

Causes of Failure

    [cite_start]
  • Lack of Military Power: The League had no army of its own to enforce decisions[cite: 700].
  • Absence of Major Powers: The United States never joined; [cite_start]Germany and the USSR were excluded for many years[cite: 700].
    • [cite_start]
  • Self-Interest: Powerful nations often prioritized their own interests over League mandates[cite: 700].

3. The Mandate System

[cite_start]

The Mandate System was a compromise between the Allied desire to retain former German and Ottoman colonies and their pledge of non-annexation[cite: 702].

[cite_start]Definition: A legal status for certain territories transferred from the control of one country to another following WWI, administered on behalf of the League of Nations[cite: 702].
    [cite_start]
  • Class A: Territories ready for near-future independence (e.g., Iraq, Palestine)[cite: 702].
    • [cite_start]
  • Class B: Territories requiring long-term administration (mostly in Africa)[cite: 702].
    • [cite_start]
  • Class C: Territories to be administered as integral parts of the mandatory power (e.g., South West Africa)[cite: 702].

4. French Quest for Security: Locarno & Kellogg-Briand

[cite_start]

France, feeling vulnerable after WWI, sought various international agreements to guarantee its borders and prevent future German aggression[cite: 703].

Locarno Agreements (1925)

    [cite_start]
  • Border Guarantee: Germany, France, and Belgium agreed to respect their mutual borders as established at Versailles[cite: 705].
    • [cite_start]
  • Spirit of Locarno: Represented a period of improved relations and hope for European peace[cite: 705].

The Kellogg-Briand Pact (1928)

    [cite_start]
  • Renunciation of War: Signatory nations promised not to use war to resolve "disputes or conflicts of whatever nature"[cite: 707].
    • [cite_start]
  • Limitation: It lacked enforcement mechanisms, making it largely symbolic[cite: 707].
Exam-Ready Enhancements

Exam Tips

When discussing the League of Nations, always highlight the absence of the USA as a primary structural weakness. For the Russian Revolution, distinguish between the February and October stages for higher marks.

Common Mistakes

    [cite_start]
  • Misinterpreting the Mandate System: Do not call them "colonies"; they were technically supervised territories[cite: 702].
    • [cite_start]
  • Overestimating Locarno: While it secured the Western border, it left Germany's Eastern borders open to future revision, which was a major flaw[cite: 705].

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What was the primary goal of the Kellogg-Briand Pact?
[cite_start]A: To outlaw war as an instrument of national policy[cite: 707].

Q: Why did the Russian Revolution happen in 1917?
[cite_start]A: A combination of Czarist autocracy, economic collapse, and the strain of World War I[cite: 699].
