1. Metamorphosis: Amphibians and Insects

[cite_start]

Metamorphosis is a biological process by which an animal physically develops after birth or hatching, involving a conspicuous and relatively abrupt change in the animal's body structure through cell growth and differentiation[cite: 1072].

Amphibian Metamorphosis

[cite_start]

Classic example: The transformation of a tadpole into an adult frog[cite: 1072].

    [cite_start]
  • Morphological Changes: Loss of tail, development of limbs, and shifting from gills to lungs[cite: 1072].
    • [cite_start]
  • Hormonal Regulation: Primarily controlled by Thyroxine (T4) and Triiodothyronine (T3) produced by the thyroid gland[cite: 1072].

Insect Metamorphosis

[cite_start]

Insects exhibit two main types of metamorphosis[cite: 1072]:

  • Hemimetabolous (Incomplete): Egg → Nymph → Adult (e.g., Grasshoppers).
  • Holometabolous (Complete): Egg → Larva → Pupa → Adult (e.g., Butterflies).
    • [cite_start]
  • Hormonal Regulation: Regulated by Ecdysone (molting hormone) and Juvenile Hormone (JH)[cite: 1072].

2. Regeneration: Modes and Mechanisms

[cite_start]

Regeneration is the process of renewal, restoration, and growth that makes genomes, cells, organisms, and ecosystems resilient to natural fluctuations or events that cause disturbance or damage[cite: 1072].

Modes of Regeneration

3. Stem Cells (ESC) and Amniocentesis

Stem Cells (ESC)

[cite_start]

Embryonic Stem Cells (ESC) are pluripotent stem cells derived from the inner cell mass of a blastocyst[cite: 1073].

    [cite_start]
  • Properties: They have the ability to differentiate into all three germ layers (ectoderm, endoderm, and mesoderm)[cite: 1073].
    • [cite_start]
  • Applications: Used in regenerative medicine, drug testing, and studying developmental biology[cite: 1073].

Amniocentesis

[cite_start]

Amniocentesis is a medical procedure used in prenatal diagnosis of chromosomal abnormalities and fetal infections[cite: 1073].

    [cite_start]
  • Procedure: A small amount of amniotic fluid, which contains fetal tissues, is sampled from the amniotic sac surrounding a developing fetus[cite: 1073].
    • [cite_start]
  • Purpose: To detect genetic disorders like Down syndrome or determine fetal lung maturity[cite: 1073].

Exam Tip: For metamorphosis, always highlight the specific hormones involved (Thyroxine for amphibians; Ecdysone/JH for insects). [cite_start]These are high-yield points for marks[cite: 1072].

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  1. Q: What is the difference between Epimorphosis and Morphallaxis?
    [cite_start]A: Epimorphosis requires a blastema and new cell growth, whereas Morphallaxis relies on reorganizing existing cells[cite: 1072].
  2. Q: Why are ESCs considered pluripotent?
    [cite_start]A: Because they can give rise to any cell type in the adult body[cite: 1073].
  3. Q: What is the primary hormone triggering molting in insects?
    [cite_start]A: Ecdysone[cite: 1072].
Common Mistake: Do not confuse Amniocentesis with Implantation. Implantation is the attachment of the embryo to the uterus; [cite_start]Amniocentesis is a diagnostic test performed much later during pregnancy[cite: 1068, 1073].

Mnemonics

  • E-P-I: Epimorphosis = Proliferation Involved (New growth).
  • M-O-R: Morphallaxis = Only Reorganizing (No blastema).
