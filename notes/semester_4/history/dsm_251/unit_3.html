2. The Bahamani Empire: Rise and Growth

[cite_start]

The Bahamani Sultanate was established in 1347 by Alauddin Bahman Shah (Hasan Gangu) as a revolt against the Tughluq rule[cite: 1731].

Key Features of Growth

    [cite_start]
  • Territorial Expansion: The kingdom spanned the Deccan from the Arabian Sea to the Bay of Bengal[cite: 1731].
    • [cite_start]
  • Administration: Divided the empire into four Tarafs (provinces): Gulbarga, Daulatabad, Berar, and Bidar[cite: 1731].
    • [cite_start]
  • Mahmud Gawan: As a brilliant Wazir, he introduced administrative reforms and built a famous Madrasa (university) at Bidar[cite: 1731].
    • [cite_start]
  • Rivalry: Constant warfare with Vijayanagara over the fertile Raichur Doab and control of diamond mines[cite: 1731].

4. Sufism and its Impact on Indian Culture

[cite_start]

Sufism, the mystical dimension of Islam, entered India around the 12th century and played a vital role in cultural synthesis[cite: 1733].

Cultural Impact

    [cite_start]
  • Religious Synthesis: Sufi saints like Nizamuddin Auliya and Moinuddin Chishti were respected by both Hindus and Muslims, bridging religious gaps[cite: 1733].
    • [cite_start]
  • Growth of Vernaculars: Sufis used local languages (Hindavi, Bengali) to spread their message, enriching regional literature[cite: 1733].
    • [cite_start]
  • Music and Poetry: The introduction of Qawwali and the use of the Sama (musical gatherings) influenced Indian musical traditions[cite: 1733].
    • [cite_start]
  • Social Equality: Sufi Khanqahs (hospices) were open to everyone regardless of caste or creed, promoting social egalitarianism[cite: 1733].

Exam Focus: Tips and FAQs

Exam Tip: Always mention Raichur Doab when discussing the Vijayanagara-Bahamani conflict. [cite_start]It was the primary economic bone of contention[cite: 1731].

Common Pitfall: Do not confuse Saguna Bhakti (with form, e.g., Chaitanya) with Nirguna Bhakti (without form, e.g., Kabir and Nanak)[cite: 1732].
