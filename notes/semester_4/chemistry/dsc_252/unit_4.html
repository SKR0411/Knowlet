1. Alkene Hydrogenation (Wilkinson’s Catalyst)

[cite_start]

Hydrogenation is the addition of hydrogen to an unsaturated organic compound in the presence of a catalyst[cite: 726].

Wilkinson’s Catalyst

    [cite_start]
  • Formula: RhCl(PPh3)3, which is a coordination complex of Rhodium[cite: 726].
    • [cite_start]
  • Type: Homogeneous catalyst (the catalyst and reactants are in the same phase)[cite: 726].
  • Mechanism:
    1. Dissociation of a phosphine ligand to create an open coordination site.
    2. Oxidative addition of dihydrogen (H2) to the metal center.
    3. Coordination of the alkene to the rhodium.
    4. Migratory insertion of the hydride into the alkene.
    5. Reductive elimination of the alkane product.

2. Hydroformylation (Oxo Process)

[cite_start]

This process involves the reaction of an alkene with carbon monoxide (CO) and hydrogen (H2) to produce aldehydes[cite: 727].

    [cite_start]
  • Catalyst: Typically uses Cobalt salts (or Rhodium complexes)[cite: 727].
    • [cite_start]
  • Importance: It is used industrially to produce high-value aldehydes that are converted into alcohols for detergents and plastics[cite: 727].
  • Key Step: CO insertion into a metal-alkyl bond to form a metal-acyl intermediate.

3. Synthetic Gasoline (Fischer-Tropsch Reaction)

[cite_start]

The Fischer-Tropsch (FT) reaction is a collection of chemical reactions that converts a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen into liquid hydrocarbons[cite: 728].

[cite_start]Overall Goal: To produce synthetic fuels (gasoline/diesel) from non-petroleum sources like coal or natural gas[cite: 728].
    [cite_start]
  • Reaction: (2n+1) H2 + n CO → CnH(2n+2) + n H2O[cite: 728].
    • [cite_start]
  • Conditions: Typically uses Iron or Cobalt catalysts at high temperatures and pressures[cite: 728].

4. Synthesis Gas by Metal Carbonyl Complexes

[cite_start]

Synthesis gas (Syngas) is a mixture consisting primarily of hydrogen, carbon monoxide, and very often some carbon dioxide[cite: 729].

    [cite_start]
  • Production: Can be generated and manipulated using metal carbonyl complexes[cite: 729].
    • [cite_start]
  • Application: Syngas serves as a building block for various chemicals including methanol and ammonia, and acts as the starting material for the Fischer-Tropsch process[cite: 728, 729].

5. Exam Focus: Tips and FAQs

Exam Tips:
    [cite_start]
  • Wilkinson's Formula: Always remember that RhCl(PPh3)3 is the active precursor for Wilkinson's catalyst[cite: 726].
    • [cite_start]
  • Mechanism Keywords: Use terms like "Oxidative Addition," "Migratory Insertion," and "Reductive Elimination" when describing organometallic catalytic cycles[cite: 726].
    • [cite_start]
  • Catalyst Metal: Remember that Hydroformylation traditionally uses Cobalt salts, whereas Wilkinson's uses Rhodium[cite: 726, 727].

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the main product of Hydroformylation?
[cite_start]A: Aldehydes are the primary product, which are then often hydrogenated to alcohols[cite: 727].

Q: Why is the Fischer-Tropsch reaction important today?
[cite_start]A: It allows for the production of liquid fuels from alternative sources like coal, biomass, and natural gas, aiding in energy security[cite: 728].

Q: What is a homogeneous catalyst?
[cite_start]A: It is a catalyst that exists in the same physical state (usually liquid solution) as the reactants, like Wilkinson's catalyst in a solvent[cite: 726].
