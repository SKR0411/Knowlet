1. Methyl Lithium (Tetramer Structure)

[cite_start]

Methyl lithium (CH3Li) is a highly reactive organometallic compound that exists as a tetrameric cluster (CH3Li)4 in the solid state and in non-coordinating solvents[cite: 719].

Structural Features

    [cite_start]
  • The four Lithium atoms form a tetrahedron[cite: 719].
    • [cite_start]
  • The methyl groups are positioned above the faces of the Li4 tetrahedron[cite: 719].
    • [cite_start]
  • Each methyl group is bonded to three Lithium atoms simultaneously, creating a multicentre bonding environment[cite: 719].

2. Trialkyl Aluminium (Dimer Structure)

[cite_start]

Trialkyl aluminium compounds, such as Trimethylaluminium (AlMe3), typically exist as dimers (Al2Me6)[cite: 719].

Structural Characteristics

    [cite_start]
  • The Aluminium atoms are connected by bridging methyl groups[cite: 719].
    • [cite_start]
  • The structure contains two types of methyl groups: terminal (attached to only one Al) and bridging (shared between two Al atoms)[cite: 719].
    • [cite_start]
  • The bridging bonds are weaker and longer than the terminal bonds[cite: 719].

3. Concept of Multicentre Bonding

[cite_start]

Multicentre bonding is a key feature in electron-deficient organometallic compounds like Al2Me6 and (LiCH3)4[cite: 719].

Definition: A type of chemical bonding where three or more atoms share a pair of electrons, often referred to as a 3-centre-2-electron (3c-2e) bond.
[cite_start]

In Al2Me6, the bridging Al-C-Al bonds are 3c-2e bonds, which allows the Aluminium atoms to achieve a more stable electronic configuration despite having fewer valence electrons than required for standard 2-centre bonds[cite: 719].

4. Ziegler-Natta Catalyst

[cite_start]

The Ziegler-Natta catalyst is a groundbreaking application of organometallic chemistry in industry, specifically for the polymerisation of ethene[cite: 720].

Composition and Role

    [cite_start]
  • It typically consists of a transition metal halide (like TiCl4) and an organometallic co-catalyst like triethylaluminium [Al(C2H5)3][cite: 720].
    • [cite_start]
  • Function: It facilitates the production of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) under relatively low pressure and temperature[cite: 720].
    • [cite_start]
  • Mechanism: The ethene molecule inserts into the metal-carbon bond, leading to a long linear polymer chain[cite: 720].

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why does Al(CH3)3 dimerize?
A: Aluminium in the monomer is electron-deficient (6 valence electrons). [cite_start]Dimerization via bridging methyl groups allows the Al atoms to reach a more stable octet-like state through 3c-2e bonds[cite: 719].

Q: What is the role of ether in Grignard reagents?
[cite_start]A: Ether acts as a Lewis base, coordinating to the Magnesium atom to stabilize the reagent through solvation[cite: 721].

Q: What is hapticity?
[cite_start]A: (Refresher from Unit 1) It describes how many contiguous atoms of a ligand are bonded to a metal center[cite: 715].
