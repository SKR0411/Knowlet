1. Blood: Components and Functions

[cite_start]Blood is a fluid connective tissue consisting of a liquid extracellular matrix called plasma and formed elements (cells and cell fragments)[cite: 633]. It acts as the primary transport medium of the body.

A. Plasma

A straw-colored liquid making up about 55% of blood volume. It contains water (90-92%), proteins (albumin, globulins, fibrinogen), electrolytes, and nutrients.

B. Formed Elements

    [cite_start]
  • Erythrocytes (RBCs): Biconcave discs lacking a nucleus; primary role is oxygen transport[cite: 633].
  • Leucocytes (WBCs): Part of the immune system. [cite_start]Divided into Granulocytes (Neutrophils, Eosinophils, Basophils) and Agranulocytes (Lymphocytes, Monocytes)[cite: 633].
    • [cite_start]
  • Thrombocytes (Platelets): Cell fragments essential for blood clotting and hemostasis[cite: 633].

2. Structure and Functions of Hemoglobin

[cite_start]Hemoglobin (Hb) is the iron-containing oxygen-transport metalloprotein in the red blood cells of all vertebrates[cite: 634].

Structure

    [cite_start]
  • Globin: A protein consisting of four polypeptide chains (two alpha and two beta chains in adults)[cite: 634].
  • Heme: Each globin chain is bound to a red pigment molecule called heme. [cite_start]Each heme group contains one iron ion (Fe2+) which can bind one oxygen molecule[cite: 634].

Functions

    [cite_start]
  • Oxygen Transport: Binds oxygen in the lungs to form Oxyhemoglobin and releases it in tissues[cite: 634].
    • [cite_start]
  • Carbon Dioxide Transport: Carries about 23% of total CO2 as carbaminohemoglobin[cite: 634].
    • [cite_start]
  • Acid-Base Balance: Acts as a protein buffer by binding to hydrogen ions[cite: 634].

3. Blood as a Buffer and Blood Groups

Blood as a Buffer System

[cite_start]Blood maintains a constant pH (around 7.4) through various buffer systems, primarily the Bicarbonate Buffer System[cite: 635].

CO2 + H2O <-> H2CO3 <-> H+ + HCO3-

4. Bone and Cartilage: Structure and Types

Cartilage

[cite_start]

A flexible, specialized connective tissue with a semi-solid matrix (chondroitin sulfate)[cite: 636].

    [cite_start]
  • Hyaline Cartilage: Most common; found in the nose, trachea, and ends of long bones[cite: 636].
    • [cite_start]
  • Elastic Cartilage: Contains elastic fibers; found in the external ear and epiglottis[cite: 636].
    • [cite_start]
  • Fibrocartilage: Strongest type; found in intervertebral discs[cite: 636].

Bone (Osseous Tissue)

[cite_start]

A hard, calcified tissue providing structural support and mineral storage[cite: 636].

    [cite_start]
  • Compact Bone: Dense outer layer consisting of Haversian systems (osteons)[cite: 636].
    • [cite_start]
  • Spongy Bone: Located inside bones; contains a lattice-like structure called trabeculae[cite: 636].

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing **Plasma** with **Serum**. Serum is plasma minus the clotting factors.
