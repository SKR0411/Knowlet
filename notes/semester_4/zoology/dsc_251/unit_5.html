1. Structural Organization of Gastrointestinal (GI) Tract

[cite_start]The GI tract follows a consistent four-layered structural pattern from the esophagus to the large intestine[cite: 648].

    [cite_start]
  • Mucosa: The innermost layer consisting of epithelium, lamina propria, and muscularis mucosae[cite: 648].
    • [cite_start]
  • Submucosa: Connective tissue containing blood vessels, lymphatics, and the submucosal (Meissnerâ€™s) nerve plexus[cite: 648].
    • [cite_start]
  • Muscularis Externa: Typically consists of an inner circular and outer longitudinal layer of smooth muscle for peristalsis[cite: 648].
    • [cite_start]
  • Serosa/Adventitia: The outermost protective layer[cite: 648].

2. Histology of Salivary Glands, Liver, Pancreas, and Lungs

Liver and Pancreas

[cite_start]

The liver is organized into hexagonal hepatic lobules[cite: 649]. [cite_start]Central veins sit at the center, while portal triads (bile duct, portal vein, hepatic artery) are at the corners[cite: 649].

[cite_start]

The pancreas has dual functions[cite: 649]:

    [cite_start]
  • Exocrine: Acinar cells secreting digestive enzymes[cite: 649].
    • [cite_start]
  • Endocrine: Islets of Langerhans secreting hormones like insulin and glucagon[cite: 649].

Lungs

[cite_start]

The functional unit is the alveolus[cite: 649]. [cite_start]The respiratory membrane consists of Type I pneumocytes (gas exchange) and Type II pneumocytes (surfactant secretion)[cite: 649].

The Heart Wall

    [cite_start]

    • [cite_start]
  • Myocardium: The thick middle layer composed of cardiac muscle cells[cite: 650].

4. Structure of Kidney and the Nephron

[cite_start]

The kidney is divided into an outer cortex and an inner medulla[cite: 651]. [cite_start]The functional unit is the Nephron[cite: 651].

The Nephron Components

    [cite_start]
  • Renal Corpuscle: Glomerulus and Bowman's capsule for filtration[cite: 651].
    • [cite_start]
  • Renal Tubule: Includes the Proximal Convoluted Tubule (PCT), Loop of Henle, and Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT)[cite: 651].

5. Exam Focus: Tips and FAQs

Exam Tips

    [cite_start]
  • Islets of Langerhans: Often asked as a short note; remember they represent the endocrine portion of the pancreas[cite: 649].
    • [cite_start]
  • Artery vs. Vein: In diagrams, arteries have a much thicker tunica media and a circular lumen compared to the collapsed lumen of veins[cite: 650].
    • [cite_start]
  • Brush Border: Mention the microvilli (brush border) in the PCT of the kidney as it is essential for absorption[cite: 651].
