1. Protozoa: General Characters, Classification, and Locomotion

[cite_start]Protozoa are microscopic, acellular (unicellular) eukaryotic organisms that exist singly or in colonies[cite: 121].

General Characters

    [cite_start]
  • They are unicellular organisms where all biological activities are performed by a single cell[cite: 121].
    • [cite_start]
  • Locomotion occurs through specialized organelles like pseudopodia, flagella, or cilia[cite: 121].
    • [cite_start]
  • Nutrition can be holophytic, holozoic, or saprozoic[cite: 121].

Classification (up to Classes)

[cite_start]

Protozoa are classified primarily based on their locomotory organelles[cite: 121]:

  • Sarcodina (Rhizopoda): Move via pseudopodia (e.g., Amoeba).
  • Mastigophora (Flagellata): Move via flagella (e.g., Euglena).
  • Ciliata: Move via cilia (e.g., Paramecium).
  • Sporozoa: Parasitic forms lacking locomotory organelles (e.g., Plasmodium).

Locomotion in Protozoa

Locomotion is essential for finding food and escaping predators. The primary mechanisms include:

    [cite_start]
  • Amoeboid movement: Streaming of cytoplasm and formation of pseudopodia[cite: 122].
    • [cite_start]
  • Flagellar movement: Whip-like action of flagella[cite: 122].
    • [cite_start]
  • Ciliary movement: Oar-like rhythmic beating of cilia[cite: 122].

2. Porifera: General Characters, Classification, and Canal System

[cite_start]Porifera, commonly known as sponges, are the simplest multicellular animals with a cellular level of organization[cite: 122].

General Characters

    [cite_start]
  • Bodies are porous with multiple inhalant pores (Ostia) and a single exhalant opening (Osculum)[cite: 122].
    • [cite_start]
  • Skeleton consists of calcareous or siliceous spicules or spongin fibers[cite: 122].
    • [cite_start]
  • Possess unique flagellated cells called choanocytes[cite: 122].

Classification (up to Classes)

  • Calcarea: Spicules are made of calcium carbonate (e.g., Sycon).
  • Hexactinellida: Six-rayed siliceous spicules (e.g., Euplectella).
  • Demospongiae: Siliceous spicules or spongin fibers (e.g., Spongilla).

Canal System in Sycon

[cite_start]The canal system is a specialized water circulatory system in sponges used for nutrition, respiration, and excretion[cite: 123].

Syconoid Canal System: Water flows through dermal ostia → Incurrent canals → Prosopyles → Radial canals (lined with choanocytes) → Apopyles → Spongocoel → [cite_start]Osculum[cite: 123].

3. Cnidaria: General Characters, Classification, and Corals

[cite_start]Cnidarians are diploblastic, tissue-grade animals characterized by the presence of stinging cells called cnidoblasts[cite: 123].

General Characters

    [cite_start]
  • Radially symmetrical body with a central gastrovascular cavity[cite: 123].
    • [cite_start]
  • Exists in two basic body forms: Polyp (sessile) and Medusa (free-swimming)[cite: 123].
    • [cite_start]
  • Metagenesis (alternation of generations) is often observed[cite: 123].

Classification (up to Classes)

  • Hydrozoa: Both polyp and medusa present (e.g., Hydra, Obelia).
  • Scyphozoa: Medusa stage is dominant (e.g., Aurelia - Jellyfish).
  • Anthozoa: Only polyp stage present; includes corals and sea anemones.

Coral and Coral Reef Formation

[cite_start]Corals are skeletal structures composed of calcium carbonate secreted by polyps of the class Anthozoa[cite: 123].

    [cite_start]
  • Fringing Reefs: Formed near the shore of a mainland or island[cite: 123].
    • [cite_start]
  • Barrier Reefs: Separated from the shore by a deep lagoon[cite: 123].
    • [cite_start]
  • Atolls: Circular reefs enclosing a central lagoon, usually formed around submerged volcanic islands[cite: 123].

4. Exam Focus Enhancements

Exam Tips

    [cite_start]
  • Choanocytes: Always mention these as the "collar cells" unique to Porifera that drive the canal system[cite: 123].
    • [cite_start]
  • Locomotion: Be ready to compare flagellar and ciliary movement; flagella are long and few, while cilia are short and numerous[cite: 122].
  • Reef Types: Barriers reefs are the largest; [cite_start]Atolls are horseshoe or circular in shape[cite: 123].

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing Ostia with Osculum. [cite_start]Ostia are for water entry (many), Osculum is for water exit (usually one)[cite: 122].
  • Thinking sponges are plants. [cite_start]Sponges are sessile animals with a cellular level of organization[cite: 122].
