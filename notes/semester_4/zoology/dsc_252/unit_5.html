1. Structure of Purines, Pyrimidines, Nucleosides, and Nucleotides

[cite_start]

Nucleic acids are the informational molecules of the cell, composed of long chains of nucleotides[cite: 682, 696].

Purines and Pyrimidines

[cite_start]

These are nitrogenous bases that form the core of nucleic acids[cite: 683].

    [cite_start]
  • Purines: Double-ring structures including Adenine (A) and Guanine (G)[cite: 683].
    • [cite_start]
  • Pyrimidines: Single-ring structures including Cytosine (C), Thymine (T) in DNA, and Uracil (U) in RNA[cite: 683].

Nucleosides vs. Nucleotides

    [cite_start]
  • Nucleoside: A nitrogenous base linked to a pentose sugar (Ribose or Deoxyribose)[cite: 683].
  • Nucleotide: A nucleoside plus one or more phosphate groups. [cite_start]Nucleotides are the monomers of DNA and RNA[cite: 683].

Base Pairing

[cite_start]

Bases pair specifically through hydrogen bonds: A pairs with T/U, and G pairs with C[cite: 683]. [cite_start]Hoogsteen base pairs are alternative base pairings that occur in certain triple-helix or specialized DNA structures[cite: 683].

2. Watson and Crick Model of B-DNA

[cite_start]

In 1953, James Watson and Francis Crick proposed the double-helix model for B-DNA[cite: 684, 696].

Key Features of B-DNA

    [cite_start]
  • Double Helix: Two polynucleotide strands wound around a common axis in a right-handed manner[cite: 684].
    • [cite_start]
  • Antiparallel Strands: One strand runs 5' to 3', while the other runs 3' to 5'[cite: 684].
    • [cite_start]
  • Sugar-Phosphate Backbone: Located on the outside of the helix[cite: 684].
    • [cite_start]
  • Internal Bases: Hydrophobic nitrogenous bases are stacked inside, perpendicular to the axis[cite: 684].

4. Denaturation, Renaturation, and Cot Curves

[cite_start]

Physical properties of DNA change with environmental conditions like heat[cite: 686].

Denaturation and Renaturation

    [cite_start]
  • Denaturation (Melting): Separation of double-stranded DNA into single strands by breaking hydrogen bonds, usually by heat or high pH[cite: 686].
    • [cite_start]
  • Renaturation (Annealing): The process where complementary single strands reform the double helix upon cooling[cite: 686].

Hyperchromaticity vs. Hypochromaticity

[cite_start]Hyperchromaticity: The increase in UV light absorption (at 260 nm) when DNA is denatured into single strands[cite: 686].
[cite_start]Hypochromaticity: The decrease in UV absorption when single strands re-anneal into a double helix[cite: 686].

Cot Curves

A Cot curve is a graph used to analyze DNA complexity. [cite_start]It plots the fraction of DNA renatured against the product of initial DNA concentration (Co) and time (t)[cite: 686]. [cite_start]Highly repetitive DNA sequences renature faster than unique sequences[cite: 686].
