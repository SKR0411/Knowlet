1. Conductivity, Mobility, and Drift Velocity

In semiconductors, the flow of charge is determined by the behavior of electrons and holes. [cite_start]Drift Velocity is the average velocity attained by charge carriers due to an electric field[cite: 405].

Mobility (μ) is defined as the magnitude of drift velocity per unit electric field. [cite_start]Conductivity (σ) of a semiconductor depends on the concentration of both electrons and holes and their respective mobilities[cite: 405].

2. Formation and Characteristics of P-N Junction Diode

[cite_start]A P-N junction is formed by joining p-type and n-type semiconductor materials[cite: 405]. At the interface, a Depletion Region is created where mobile charge carriers are absent.

[cite_start]The V-I Characteristics show how current varies with applied voltage[cite: 405]. In forward bias, current increases exponentially after reaching the Knee Voltage. [cite_start]In reverse bias, only a very small leakage current flows until breakdown occurs[cite: 405].

3. Current Flow Mechanism in Biasing

    [cite_start]
  • Forward Bias: The positive terminal is connected to the p-side[cite: 405]. [cite_start]This narrows the depletion region, allowing majority carriers to cross the junction easily[cite: 406].
    • [cite_start]
  • Reverse Bias: The positive terminal is connected to the n-side[cite: 405]. [cite_start]This widens the depletion region, preventing majority carrier flow and allowing only minority carrier drift[cite: 406].

4. Static and Dynamic Resistance

[cite_start]The resistance of a diode is not constant because its V-I curve is non-linear[cite: 406].

  • Static (DC) Resistance: The resistance at a specific DC operating point. [cite_start]R = V / I[cite: 406].
    • [cite_start]
  • Dynamic (AC) Resistance: The resistance offered to a changing signal, calculated as the reciprocal of the slope of the V-I curve. r = ΔV / ΔI[cite: 406].

6. Filter Circuits (C and π-filter)

[cite_start]Filters are used to remove the AC components (ripples) from the rectifier output to provide smooth DC[cite: 407].

  • C-Filter (Capacitor Filter): A capacitor is connected in parallel with the load. [cite_start]It charges during the peak and discharges through the load, smoothing the voltage[cite: 407].
    • [cite_start]
  • π-Filter: Consists of two capacitors and an inductor (or resistor) in a 'π' shape, providing superior ripple reduction compared to a simple C-filter[cite: 407].

7. Zener Diode and Voltage Regulation

[cite_start]A Zener Diode is a heavily doped diode designed to operate in the reverse breakdown region without damage[cite: 408].

Breakdown Mechanisms:

    [cite_start]
  • Zener Breakdown: Occurs in heavily doped junctions at low voltages due to high electric fields pulling electrons from valence bands[cite: 408].
    • [cite_start]
  • Avalanche Breakdown: Occurs in lightly doped junctions at high voltages due to collision-induced ionization[cite: 408].
[cite_start]

Voltage Regulation: The Zener diode maintains a constant voltage across the load even if the input voltage or load current fluctuates[cite: 408].

8. LED and Solar Cell

    [cite_start]
  • LED (Light Emitting Diode): A forward-biased P-N junction that emits light when electrons recombine with holes, releasing energy as photons[cite: 408].
    • [cite_start]
  • Solar Cell: A P-N junction that converts light energy directly into electrical energy through the photovoltaic effect[cite: 408].

Exam Focus Corner

Frequently Asked Questions

    [cite_start]
  • Define Ripple Factor. It is the ratio of the RMS value of the AC component to the DC component in the rectifier output[cite: 407].
    • [cite_start]
  • How does a Zener diode act as a regulator? In the breakdown region, the voltage across the Zener remains constant over a wide range of current[cite: 408].

Common Mistakes

  • Biasing: Confusing forward and reverse bias connections. Remember: P to Positive = Forward.
  • Efficiency: Forgetting that full-wave efficiency is exactly double that of half-wave.

Exam Tips

Tip: Always draw the V-I characteristics when explaining diodes. Use bold labels for the Knee Voltage and Breakdown Voltage. For rectifiers, the output waveforms are just as important as the circuit diagram.
