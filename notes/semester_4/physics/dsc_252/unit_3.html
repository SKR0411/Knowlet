1. Hybrid Parameter (h-parameter) and Equivalent Circuit

[cite_start]Hybrid parameters, or h-parameters, are used to represent a transistor as a two-port network for small-signal AC analysis[cite: 419]. [cite_start]They are called "hybrid" because they use a mixture of units (Ohms, Mhos, and unitless ratios)[cite: 419].

The four h-parameters for a Common Emitter (CE) configuration are:

    [cite_start]
  • hie: Input impedance (Ohms)[cite: 419].
    • [cite_start]
  • hre: Reverse voltage gain (unitless)[cite: 419].
    • [cite_start]
  • hfe: Forward current gain (unitless)[cite: 419].
    • [cite_start]
  • hoe: Output admittance (Mhos)[cite: 419].

2. Analysis of CE Amplifier using h-parameters

[cite_start]Using the h-parameter equivalent circuit, we can derive expressions for the performance of a CE amplifier[cite: 419]:

    [cite_start]
  • Current Gain (Ai): The ratio of output current to input current[cite: 419].
    • [cite_start]
  • Voltage Gain (Av): The ratio of output voltage to input voltage[cite: 419].
    • [cite_start]
  • Input Impedance (Zi): The resistance seen by the input signal[cite: 419].
    • [cite_start]
  • Output Impedance (Zo): The resistance seen looking back into the output terminals[cite: 419].

3. Frequency Response of Two-Stage R-C Coupled Amplifier

[cite_start]In multi-stage amplifiers, stages are connected via a capacitor and a resistor (R-C coupling)[cite: 420].

[cite_start]The frequency response is a plot of voltage gain versus frequency[cite: 420]. [cite_start]It remains constant over a specific range called the Bandwidth (BW)[cite: 420]. [cite_start]Gain drops at low frequencies due to coupling capacitors and at high frequencies due to internal transistor capacitances[cite: 420].

4. Sinusoidal Oscillators and Barkhausen's Criterion

[cite_start]An oscillator is a circuit that produces a continuous, periodic output signal without an external input[cite: 421].

[cite_start]

For self-sustained oscillations, the Barkhausen's Criterion must be satisfied[cite: 421]:

1. The loop gain (product of amplifier gain A and feedback fraction β) must be equal to unity: |Aβ| [cite_start]= 1[cite: 421].
2. [cite_start]The total phase shift around the loop must be 0° or 360°[cite: 421].

5. RC Phase Shift Oscillator

[cite_start]The RC Phase Shift Oscillator uses an RC network in the feedback loop to provide the required 180° phase shift[cite: 421]. [cite_start]Since the CE amplifier already provides 180°, the total loop phase shift becomes 360°[cite: 421].

[cite_start]

The frequency of oscillation is determined by the R and C values in the feedback network[cite: 421].

6. Operational Amplifiers (IC-741): Characteristics

[cite_start]An Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) is a high-gain DC-coupled electronic voltage amplifier with differential inputs[cite: 422]. [cite_start]The IC-741 is the most common practical Op-Amp[cite: 422].

Key Parameters:

    [cite_start]
  • CMRR (Common Mode Rejection Ratio): Ability to reject signals common to both inputs[cite: 422].
    • [cite_start]
  • Slew Rate: Maximum rate of change of output voltage per unit time[cite: 422].
    • [cite_start]
  • Virtual Ground: A concept where the inverting terminal is at zero potential if the non-inverting terminal is grounded, provided the open-loop gain is infinite[cite: 422].

7. Applications of Op-Amp

[cite_start]

By using different feedback networks, Op-Amps can perform mathematical operations[cite: 423]:

    [cite_start]
  • Adder (Summing Amplifier): Produces an output proportional to the sum of input voltages[cite: 423].
    • [cite_start]
  • Differentiator: Produces an output proportional to the rate of change of the input voltage[cite: 423].
    • [cite_start]
  • Integrator: Produces an output proportional to the integral of the input voltage over time[cite: 423].
    • [cite_start]
  • Inverting/Non-inverting Amplifiers: Basic configurations for signal amplification[cite: 423].

Exam Focus Corner

Frequently Asked Questions

    [cite_start]
  • State the ideal characteristics of an Op-Amp. [cite: 422] [cite_start]
    Infinite gain, infinite input impedance, zero output impedance, infinite bandwidth, and infinite CMRR[cite: 422].
    • [cite_start]
  • Why is the RC Phase Shift Oscillator used for low frequencies? [cite: 421] [cite_start]
    Because at high frequencies, the size of capacitors required becomes too small, making the circuit unstable[cite: 421].

Common Mistakes

  • Virtual Ground: Applying the "Virtual Ground" concept to non-inverting configurations. [cite_start]It only applies to the inverting input when the non-inverting input is grounded[cite: 422].
  • Oscillators: Confusing the phase shift of the amplifier with the phase shift of the feedback network. [cite_start]Both must sum to 360°[cite: 421].

Exam Tips

[cite_start]Tip: When deriving Op-Amp formulas (Adder/Integrator), always start by stating that the input current to the Op-Amp is zero due to infinite input impedance[cite: 422, 423]. [cite_start]This simplifies the Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL) analysis at the inverting node[cite: 423].
