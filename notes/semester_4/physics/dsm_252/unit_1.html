1. Electrostatic Field and Electric Flux

[cite_start]The Electrostatic Field is the region around a charged particle where another charge experience a force[cite: 1499]. [cite_start]Electric Flux is a measure of the total number of electric field lines passing through a given area[cite: 1499].

2. Gauss's Theorem and Applications

[cite_start]Gauss's Theorem states that the total electric flux through any closed surface is equal to 1/ε₀ times the net charge enclosed by that surface[cite: 1499].

Applications:

    [cite_start]
  • Point Charge: Determining the field at a distance r[cite: 1499].
    • [cite_start]
  • Infinite Line of Charge: Field due to a wire with linear charge density λ[cite: 1499].
    • [cite_start]
  • Uniformly Charged Spherical Shell and Solid Sphere: Analyzing the field both inside and outside the sphere[cite: 1499].
    • [cite_start]
  • Plane Charged Sheet: Field due to an infinitely large flat surface[cite: 1499].

3. Electric Potential

[cite_start]Electric Potential at a point is defined as the work done in bringing a unit positive charge from infinity to that point[cite: 1500]. [cite_start]It can be expressed as the negative line integral of the electric field[cite: 1500].

[cite_start]V = - ∫ E · dl [cite: 1500]

4. Potential due to an Electric Dipole

[cite_start]An Electric Dipole consists of two equal and opposite charges separated by a small distance[cite: 1500]. [cite_start]The potential at any point in the field of a dipole depends on the dipole moment and the distance/orientation of the point[cite: 1500].

5. Capacitance and Condensers

[cite_start]Capacitance is the ability of a conductor to store electric charge[cite: 1501]. [cite_start]A Condenser (Capacitor) is a system of two conductors used to store large amounts of charge and energy[cite: 1501].

Types of Capacitors:

    [cite_start]
  • Isolated Spherical Conductor: Capacitance depends on its radius[cite: 1501].
    • [cite_start]
  • Parallel Plate Condenser: Most common type; capacitance depends on plate area and separation[cite: 1501].
    • [cite_start]
  • Spherical and Cylindrical Condensers: Used for specific engineering and physical applications[cite: 1501].

6. Energy in Electrostatic Field

Energy is required to assemble a system of charges. [cite_start]This energy is stored in the Electrostatic Field itself[cite: 1501]. [cite_start]The Energy per unit volume (Energy Density) in an electrostatic field is a key concept in electromagnetism[cite: 1501].
