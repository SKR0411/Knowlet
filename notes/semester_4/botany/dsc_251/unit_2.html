Cereals: Rice and Wheat

[cite_start]

Cereals are members of the grass family (Poaceae) cultivated for their edible starchy seeds[cite: 650]. They provide the bulk of the world's calorie intake.

Rice (Oryza sativa)

    [cite_start]
  • Origin: South-East Asia (Indo-Burma region)[cite: 650].
  • Morphology: Annual aquatic grass with a fibrous root system. [cite_start]The inflorescence is a panicle[cite: 650]. The fruit is a caryopsis, where the seed coat is fused with the fruit wall.
    • [cite_start]
  • Post-Harvest Management: Involves drying, cleaning, hulling (removing the husk), and polishing[cite: 650].
    • [cite_start]
  • Uses: Primary staple food; used to make flour, flakes (poha), and fermented products like rice wine[cite: 650].

Wheat (Triticum aestivum)

    [cite_start]
  • Origin: South-West Asia (Near East/Levant)[cite: 650].
  • Morphology: Erect annual grass. [cite_start]The inflorescence is a spike of spikelets[cite: 650]. Common bread wheat is hexaploid (6n).
    • [cite_start]
  • Processing: Milling involves cleaning, tempering, and grinding to separate the bran, germ, and endosperm[cite: 650].
    • [cite_start]
  • Uses: Primarily for bread, pasta, and pastries due to gluten content[cite: 650].

Millets and Nutritional Values

[cite_start]

Millets are a group of small-seeded grasses grown as cereal crops or grains for fodder and human food[cite: 650].

    [cite_start]
  • Brief Account: Includes Pearl Millet (Bajra), Finger Millet (Ragi), and Sorghum (Jowar)[cite: 650].
    • [cite_start]
  • Nutritional Values: They are rich in fiber, minerals (Calcium, Iron), and vitamins[cite: 650]. [cite_start]They are gluten-free and have a low glycemic index, making them ideal for diabetics[cite: 650].

Legumes: Chick pea and Pigeon pea

[cite_start]

Legumes (Family: Fabaceae) are essential for protein in vegetarian diets[cite: 651].

Chick pea (Cicer arietinum)

    [cite_start]
  • Origin: South-West Asia[cite: 651].
  • Morphology: Small, herbaceous plant with pinnately compound leaves. [cite_start]Pods contain 1-2 seeds[cite: 651].
    • [cite_start]
  • Uses: Consumed as whole seeds, split (dal), or ground into flour (besan)[cite: 651].

Pigeon pea (Cajanus cajan)

    [cite_start]
  • Origin: India[cite: 651].
  • Morphology: Perennial woody shrub. Leaves are trifoliate. [cite_start]Pods are hairy and contain 2-9 seeds[cite: 651].
    • [cite_start]
  • Uses: Primarily consumed as Arhar/Tur dal[cite: 651].

Beverages: Tea and Coffee

[cite_start]

Non-alcoholic beverages that act as stimulants due to alkaloids like caffeine[cite: 652].

Tea (Camellia sinensis)

    [cite_start]
  • Morphology: Evergreen shrub with serrated leaves[cite: 652].
    • [cite_start]
  • Cultivation: Requires acidic soil (pH 4.5-5.5) and high rainfall[cite: 652].
    • [cite_start]
  • Processing: Withering, Rolling, Fermentation (Oxidation), and Firing/Drying[cite: 652].

Coffee (Coffea arabica / Coffea canephora)

    [cite_start]
  • Morphology: Small evergreen trees with glossy leaves and white flowers[cite: 652].
    • [cite_start]
  • Processing: Removal of pulp (wet or dry method), fermentation, hulling, roasting, and grinding[cite: 652].

Exam Tips
    [cite_start]
  • Origin Questions: Always link Rice to SE Asia and Wheat to SW Asia[cite: 650].
    • [cite_start]
  • Nutritional Values: Emphasize the high protein content in legumes and mineral content in millets[cite: 650, 651].
    • [cite_start]
  • Tea Processing: "Withering" and "Firing" are specific keywords that examiners look for[cite: 652].
Common Mistakes
    [cite_start]
  • Identifying Saffron as a seed; it is the stigma of the flower[cite: 654].
    • [cite_start]
  • Confusing the family of cereals (Poaceae) with legumes (Fabaceae)[cite: 650, 651].
Mnemonic for Tea Processing: WRFF (Withering, Rolling, Fermentation, Firing).
