1. Archegoniate: Morphological and Reproductive Studies

[cite_start]

This section involves the identification and microscopic study of vegetative and reproductive structures through temporary and permanent slides[cite: 707, 708].

Bryophytes

    [cite_start]
  • Genera to Study: Marchantia, Anthoceros, Sphagnum, and Polytrichum[cite: 708].
    • [cite_start]
  • Focus: Observation of thallus organization, sex organs (antheridia/archegonia), and sporophyte structures[cite: 708].

Pteridophytes

    [cite_start]
  • Genera to Study: Psilotum, Lycopodium, Selaginella, and Marsilea[cite: 709].
    • [cite_start]
  • Focus: Internal and external anatomy of vegetative parts and reproductive organs (sporangia/strobili)[cite: 709].

Gymnosperms

    [cite_start]
  • Genera to Study: Cycas, Pinus, and Gnetum[cite: 710].
    • [cite_start]
  • Focus: Study of vegetative anatomy (e.g., coralloid roots, needle anatomy) and reproductive structures like male and female cones[cite: 710].

2. Genetics, Molecular Biology & Plant Breeding

[cite_start]

Practical application of theoretical genetic principles and molecular techniques[cite: 711].

Classical Genetics and Human Traits

    [cite_start]
  • Mendelian Genetics: Verification of laws using seed ratios (e.g., 3:1 or 9:3:3:1)[cite: 712].
    • [cite_start]
  • Aneuploidy: Study of chromosomal disorders including Down's, Klinefelter's, and Turner's syndromes using photographs[cite: 713].
    • [cite_start]
  • Human Genetic Traits: Observation of traits such as Sickle cell anemia, Xeroderma pigmentosum, Albinism, Color blindness, Widow's peak, Rolling tongue, and attached ear lobes[cite: 714].

Molecular Biology Techniques

    [cite_start]
  • DNA Isolation: Extraction of genomic DNA from plant tissues and bacteria[cite: 715, 716].
    • [cite_start]
  • Replication and Transcription: Visual study of mechanisms (rolling circle, theta replication) and RNA polymerase through photographs[cite: 717, 718].

Plant Breeding

    [cite_start]
  • Emasculation: Practicing the removal of anthers from bisexual flowers (e.g., Datura or Pea) to prevent self-pollination[cite: 719].
    • [cite_start]
  • Hybridization: Demonstration and practice of crossing techniques[cite: 720].

Exam Tips & Guidelines
  • Drawing: Ensure all morphological sketches are clear and labeled. In systematics, the floral formula and diagram are mandatory for full marks.
  • Chemical Tests: For grains and pulses, remember that iodine is used to test for starch and Biuret or Xanthoproteic tests for proteins.
  • Herbarium Accuracy: Ensure your labels include the Botanical name, Family, Locality, and Date of Collection.
  • Identification: For Archegoniates, focus on "identifying characters" that distinguish one genus from another (e.g., the sporophyte shape in Anthoceros).
