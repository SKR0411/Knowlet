Introduction to Plant Growth Regulators

[cite_start]

Plant Growth Regulators (PGRs), often called phytohormones, are small, simple chemical molecules that regulate plant growth, development, and responses to the environment[cite: 779, 783]. [cite_start]These substances are produced in one part of the plant and typically translocated to another part to exert their physiological effects[cite: 780, 783].

Auxins: Discovery and Physiological Roles

[cite_start]

Auxins are primarily produced in the shoot apical meristems and young leaves[cite: 780].

    [cite_start]
  • Discovery: Initial observations were made by Charles Darwin and his son Francis during their work on phototropism in canary grass coleoptiles[cite: 780].
    • [cite_start]
  • Chemical Nature: The most common natural auxin is Indole-3-acetic acid (IAA)[cite: 780]. [cite_start]Synthetic auxins include 2,4-D and NAA[cite: 780].
  • Physiological Roles:
      [cite_start]
    • Promotes cell elongation and apical dominance[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Initiates root formation in stem cuttings[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Prevents premature fruit and leaf drop (abscission)[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Induces parthenocarpy (seedless fruit production) in some plants[cite: 780].

Gibberellins: Nature and Function

[cite_start]

Gibberellins are a group of acidic plant hormones[cite: 780].

    [cite_start]
  • Discovery: Identified first in Japan in relation to the "bakanae" (foolish seedling) disease of rice caused by the fungus Gibberella fujikuroi[cite: 780].
    • [cite_start]
  • Chemical Nature: There are over 100 known gibberellins, with Gibberellic Acid (GA3) being the most studied[cite: 780].
  • Physiological Roles:
      [cite_start]
    • Promotes stem elongation and increases the length of stalks in grapes[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Stimulates seed germination by breaking dormancy[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Promotes "bolting" (internode elongation) in rosette plants like cabbage[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Delays senescence in fruits[cite: 780].

Cytokinins: Promoting Cell Division

[cite_start]

Cytokinins are hormones specifically associated with cytokinesis (cell division)[cite: 780].

    [cite_start]
  • Discovery: Discovered during attempts to culture tobacco pith tissue, where they were found to promote growth in the presence of auxin[cite: 780]. [cite_start]Kinetin was the first cytokinin identified[cite: 780].
  • Physiological Roles:
      [cite_start]
    • Promotes active cell division in roots, shoots, and developing fruits[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Helps overcome apical dominance by promoting lateral bud growth[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Delays leaf senescence by promoting nutrient mobilization[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Induces chloroplast development in leaves[cite: 780].

Abscisic Acid (ABA): The Stress Hormone

[cite_start]

ABA acts primarily as a growth inhibitor and plays a critical role in plant responses to stress[cite: 780].

    [cite_start]
  • Chemical Nature: A terpene-derived compound[cite: 780].
  • Physiological Roles:
      [cite_start]
    • Induces stomatal closure during water stress to prevent transpiration[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Promotes seed and bud dormancy[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Increases plant tolerance to various kinds of stresses[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Promotes leaf abscission and senescence[cite: 780].

Ethylene: The Gaseous Hormone

[cite_start]

Ethylene is unique among phytohormones because it is a simple gaseous molecule[cite: 780].

    [cite_start]
  • Discovery: Identified as the substance released from ripe oranges that accelerated the ripening of stored bananas[cite: 780].
  • Physiological Roles:
      [cite_start]
    • Promotes horizontal growth of seedlings and swelling of the axis[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Highly effective in fruit ripening (e.g., tomatoes, apples)[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Promotes senescence and abscission of leaves and flowers[cite: 780].
      • [cite_start]
    • Breaks seed and bud dormancy and initiates flowering in pineapples[cite: 780].

Exam Tips
    [cite_start]
  • The "Bakanae" Disease: Always associate this with the discovery of Gibberellins[cite: 780].
    • [cite_start]
  • Apical Dominance: Remember that Auxin *promotes* it while Cytokinin *overcomes* it[cite: 780].
    • [cite_start]
  • Stress Hormone: ABA is the definitive answer for questions regarding stomatal closure and stress tolerance[cite: 780].
    • [cite_start]
  • Parthenocarpy: Be prepared to list which hormones (Auxin/GA) can induce seedless fruit[cite: 780].
Common Pitfalls
  • Confusing Ethylene with Ethane; [cite_start]Ethylene (C2H4) is the gaseous hormone[cite: 780].
    • [cite_start]
  • Thinking ABA only promotes abscission; its role in seed dormancy and stomatal control is actually more significant[cite: 780].
  • Mistaking IAA for a synthetic hormone; [cite_start]IAA is the primary *natural* auxin[cite: 780].
