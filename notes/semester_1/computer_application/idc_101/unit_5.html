E-mail: Structure and Usage

[cite_start]

Electronic mail is one of the most widely used IT services for professional and personal communication[cite: 173].

Structure of an E-mail

    [cite_start]
  • Headers: Includes "To" (primary recipients), "CC" (Carbon Copy for visibility), and "BCC" (Blind Carbon Copy for private recipients)[cite: 173].
    • [cite_start]
  • Subject Line: A brief description of the email's content[cite: 173].
    • [cite_start]
  • Body: The main message area[cite: 173].
    • [cite_start]
  • Attachments: Files like documents or images sent with the message[cite: 173].

E-mail Operations

[cite_start]

Common tasks include Opening, Creating, Sending, Forwarding, and Replying to messages[cite: 173].

E-commerce and Netiquettes

e-Governance Services and Digital Locker

    [cite_start]
  • e-Governance: Using IT to deliver government services to citizens efficiently[cite: 173]. [cite_start]Many of these services are now available on mobile devices[cite: 173].
    • [cite_start]
  • Digital Locker (DigiLocker): A platform for the issuance and verification of documents and certificates in a digital way, eliminating the use of physical documents[cite: 173].

Digital Financial Tools

[cite_start]

IT has revolutionized finance through various digital tools that make transactions faster and more secure[cite: 174].

    [cite_start]
  • OTP (One Time Password): A secure, temporary code for authenticating a single transaction[cite: 174].
    • [cite_start]
  • QR Code: A machine-readable code used for instant payments by scanning[cite: 174].
    • [cite_start]
  • UPI (Unified Payments Interface): A real-time payment system for instant bank transfers on mobile[cite: 174].
    • [cite_start]
  • eWallet: A digital account used to store money for online and offline purchases[cite: 174].
    • [cite_start]
  • PoS (Point of Sale): Physical terminals used at retail stores to process card payments[cite: 174].

Exam Focus & Tips

    [cite_start]
  • Exam Tip: Be ready to explain the difference between CC and BCC in an e-mail structure. [cite: 173]
    • [cite_start]
  • Mnemonic: "RIN" (RTGS, IMPS, NEFT) to remember the three major bank transfer modes. [cite: 174]
  • Common Mistake: Confusing e-Governance with e-Commerce. Remember: Governance is for government services; [cite_start]Commerce is for buying/selling. [cite: 173]

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the benefit of a Digital Locker?
[cite_start]A: It allows you to store and share authentic digital documents, reducing the need for physical copies[cite: 173].

Q: What is a QR Code?
[cite_start]A: It stands for Quick Response code, used to share information or process payments instantly[cite: 174].
