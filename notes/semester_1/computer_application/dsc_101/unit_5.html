Using E-mails and Structure

[cite_start]

Electronic Mail (E-mail) is a method of exchanging messages between people using electronic devices via the internet[cite: 26].

Structure of an E-mail

    [cite_start]
  • Header: Contains the sender's address, recipient's address (To), carbon copy (CC), blind carbon copy (BCC), and the Subject line[cite: 26].
    • [cite_start]
  • Body: The actual content of the message[cite: 26].
    • [cite_start]
  • Attachments: Files (images, documents) sent along with the mail[cite: 26].
    • [cite_start]
  • Signature: Professional contact details automatically added at the end[cite: 26].

Social Networking & Blogs

[cite_start]

Platforms that facilitate the creation and sharing of information, ideas, and interests via virtual communities[cite: 26].

    [cite_start]
  • Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram: Popular social platforms for personal and professional networking[cite: 26].
    • [cite_start]
  • Instant Messaging: Real-time text transmission over the internet[cite: 26].
    • [cite_start]
  • Blogs: Online journals or informational websites displaying information in reverse chronological order[cite: 26].

E-commerce and Netiquettes

[cite_start]E-commerce: The buying and selling of goods and services, or the transmitting of funds or data, over an electronic network, primarily the internet[cite: 26].

Netiquettes

[cite_start]

Short for "network etiquette," these are the informal rules of behavior for respectful communication online[cite: 26]. [cite_start]Examples include avoiding "SHOUTING" (all caps), respecting privacy, and using professional language[cite: 26].

e-Governance & Digital Locker

    [cite_start]
  • e-Governance: The application of IT for delivering government services and exchange of information between government and citizens[cite: 26].
    • [cite_start]
  • Digital Locker (DigiLocker): A flagship initiative for providing citizens with a shareable private space on a public cloud to store and verify documents/certificates[cite: 26].

Exam Focus & Tips

    [cite_start]
  • Exam Tip: Be prepared to explain the difference between CC (Carbon Copy) and BCC (Blind Carbon Copy) in E-mails[cite: 26].
  • Common Mistake: Confusing NEFT with RTGS. Remember: RTGS is for large, real-time transfers; [cite_start]NEFT works in batches[cite: 27].
    • [cite_start]
  • Mnemonic: "QUICK" for financial tools - QR, UPI, IMPS, Cards, Knowledge of OTP[cite: 27].
