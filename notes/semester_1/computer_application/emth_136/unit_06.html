1. Arithmetic Progression (A.P.)

A sequence is an Arithmetic Progression if the difference between any two consecutive terms is constant. This constant difference is called the Common Difference ($d$).

General Term ($n^{th}$ term): $a_n = a + (n - 1)d$
Sum of $n$ terms ($S_n$): $S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n - 1)d]$ OR $S_n = \frac{n}{2}[a + l]$

Where $a$ = first term, $d$ = common difference, $n$ = number of terms, and $l$ = last term.

2. Geometric Progression (G.P.)

A sequence is a Geometric Progression if the ratio of any two consecutive terms is constant. This constant ratio is called the Common Ratio ($r$).

General Term ($n^{th}$ term): $a_n = ar^{n-1}$
Sum of $n$ terms ($S_n$):
If $r \neq 1$, $S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1}$ (for $r > 1$) OR $S_n = \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}$ (for $r < 1$)

Sum of Infinite G.P.: If $|r| < 1$, the sum of an infinite G.P. is $S_{\infty} = \frac{a}{1 - r}$.

3. Arithmetic Mean (A.M.) and Geometric Mean (G.M.)

Means are values inserted between two numbers to form a progression.

  • Single A.M. between $a$ and $b$: $A = \frac{a + b}{2}$
  • Single G.M. between $a$ and $b$: $G = \sqrt{ab}$
Property: For any two positive real numbers $a$ and $b$, $A.M. \geq G.M.$

4. Summation of Special Series

These standard results are used to find the sum of series that are not directly in A.P. or G.P.

Sum of first $n$ natural numbers: $\sum n = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2}$
Sum of squares: $\sum n^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6}$
Sum of cubes: $\sum n^3 = \left[ \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \right]^2$

5. Exam Focus Enhancements

Exam Tips
  • Selection of Terms: If you need to assume 3 terms in A.P., use $(a-d), a, (a+d)$. For G.P., use $\frac{a}{r}, a, ar$. This simplifies calculations significantly.
  • Infinite G.P.: This only works if $|r| < 1$. If $r \geq 1$, the sum is infinite.
  • Method of Difference: For complex series, find the $n^{th}$ term ($T_n$) first, then apply the $\sum$ operator to find $S_n$.
Common Mistakes
  • Confusing $d$ and $r$: Ensure you subtract for A.P. ($a_2 - a_1$) and divide for G.P. ($a_2 / a_1$).
  • $n$ vs $a_n$: $n$ is the position (index), while $a_n$ is the value at that position.
  • Sum of Squares Formula: Forgetting the $(2n+1)$ part of the $\sum n^2$ formula is a very common error.
Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can $d$ or $r$ be negative?
A: Yes. A negative $d$ means a decreasing A.P. A negative $r$ means the signs of the terms will alternate.

Q: What is the relationship between A.M. and G.M.?
A: $A.M. \geq G.M.$. They are equal only if the numbers $a$ and $b$ are equal.
