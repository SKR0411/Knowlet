1. Slope of a Line

The Slope (or gradient) of a line is a measure of its steepness and direction. It is denoted by $m$.

  • Using Inclination: If $\theta$ is the angle a line makes with the positive x-axis, then $m = \tan \theta$.
  • Using Two Points: If a line passes through $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, then:
    $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$

Conditions: Parallel lines have equal slopes ($m_1 = m_2$). Perpendicular lines have a product of slopes equal to $-1$ ($m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$).

2. Angle Between Two Lines

When two lines with slopes $m_1$ and $m_2$ intersect, the acute angle $\alpha$ between them is given by:

$\tan \alpha = \left| \frac{m_2 - m_1}{1 + m_1 m_2} \right|$

3. Various Forms of Equations of a Line

Depending on the information available, we use different forms to represent a straight line in the Cartesian plane.

Form Name Equation Given Information
Slope-Intercept Form $y = mx + c$ Slope ($m$) and y-intercept ($c$).
Point-Slope Form $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ Slope ($m$) and a point $(x_1, y_1)$.
Two-Point Form $y - y_1 = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}(x - x_1)$ Two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$.
Intercept Form $\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1$ x-intercept ($a$) and y-intercept ($b$).

4. Distance of a Point from a Line

The perpendicular distance $d$ from a point $(x_1, y_1)$ to a line $Ax + By + C = 0$ is calculated as:

$d = \frac{|Ax_1 + By_1 + C|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$

Distance between two parallel lines $Ax + By + C_1 = 0$ and $Ax + By + C_2 = 0$ is:

$d = \frac{|C_1 - C_2|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$

5. Exam Focus Enhancements

Exam Tips
  • The Intercept Trick: If you are asked to find the intercepts of $3x + 4y = 12$, simply divide the whole equation by $12$ to get $\frac{x}{4} + \frac{y}{3} = 1$. The intercepts are $(4, 0)$ and $(0, 3)$.
  • Collinearity: Three points are collinear if the slope of $AB$ equals the slope of $BC$.
  • General Form: Always try to leave your final answer in the general form $Ax + By + C = 0$.
Common Mistakes
  • Vertical Line Slope: Remember that the slope of a vertical line is undefined ($\tan 90^\circ$).
  • Sign Errors: When using the distance formula, ensure all terms are on one side of the equation ($= 0$) before identifying $A, B,$ and $C$.
  • Intercept Form: The intercept form must have a "$+1$" on the right side. If it's $-1$, multiply the whole equation by $-1$.
Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I find the slope of a line given in general form $Ax + By + C = 0$?
A: Convert it to $y = mx + c$ form. The slope is $m = -A/B$.

Q: What is the slope of the x-axis and y-axis?
A: The x-axis has a slope of $0$ ($\tan 0^\circ$). The y-axis has an undefined slope ($\tan 90^\circ$).
