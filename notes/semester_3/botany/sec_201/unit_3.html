1. Concepts

  • Pomology: Science of growing fruit. Includes site selection, planting, care, and harvesting.
  • Floriculture: Science of growing flowers for commercial (cut flowers, loose flowers) and aesthetic purposes.

2. Important Flower Crops

A. Rose ("Queen of Flowers")

  • Scientific Name: Rosa spp.
  • Propagation: T-Budding and Hardwood Cuttings.
  • Pruning: Essential operation done in Oct-Nov (North India) to induce fresh flowering shoots.
  • Uses: Cut flowers, Gulkand, Rose oil (Attar).

B. Orchids

  • Features: Epiphytic or terrestrial. Known for long vase life and exotic shapes.
  • Important Genera: Dendrobium, Cymbidium, Vanda.
  • Cultivation: Require high humidity and shade. Growing media usually includes charcoal, brick pieces, and coconut husk (not soil).

3. Important Fruit Crops

Crop Scientific Name Propagation Key Points
Mango Mangifera indica Veneer Grafting / Inarching "King of Fruits". Alternate bearing is a major problem (fruits every alternate year).
Banana Musa sp. Suckers (Sword suckers) Monocarpic (fruits once then dies). Propagated via rhizomes/suckers. High potassium requirement.
Citrus Citrus spp. (Lemon, Orange) T-Budding / Air Layering Rich in Vitamin C. Susceptible to Citrus Canker (bacterial disease).
Pineapple Ananas comosus Suckers / Slips / Crowns CAM plant (drought tolerant). Flowering can be induced artificially using Ethrel.

4. Orchard Management

  • Layout Systems: Square system (most common), Hexagonal (15% more trees), Contour (for hills).
  • Floor Management: Clean cultivation (weeding), Sod culture (growing grass), or Mulching (covering soil to save water).
  • Pruning vs. Training:
    Training: Done in early years to give shape to the tree.
    Pruning: Removal of diseased/unwanted branches to encourage fruiting.
