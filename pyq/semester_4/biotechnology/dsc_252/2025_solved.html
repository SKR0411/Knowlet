UNIT-I

Question 1(a): Define bioprocess technology. 2 Marks

[span_2](start_span)

Bioprocess technology is the application of engineering and biological principles to transform biological materials (like cells or enzymes) into useful products, such as pharmaceuticals, food, and biofuels[span_2](end_span). It integrates biology and chemical engineering to create efficient large-scale production systems.

Question 1(b): What is fed-batch culture? 2 Marks

[span_3](start_span)

A fed-batch culture is a biotechnological production process where one or more nutrients are added to the bioreactor during the cultivation[span_3](end_span). [span_4](start_span)Unlike a standard batch culture, the product remains in the vessel until the end of the run, allowing for higher cell densities and product yields[span_4](end_span).

Question 1(c): What is continuous culture? 2 Marks

[span_5](start_span)

Continuous culture is a system where fresh medium is added continuously to the bioreactor, while an equal volume of culture (including cells and waste products) is simultaneously removed[span_5](end_span). [span_6](start_span)This maintains the microbial population in a steady-state exponential growth phase[span_6](end_span).

(i) Batch culture:

[span_19](start_span)

In a batch culture, a fixed volume of medium is inoculated with a microorganism in a closed system[span_19](end_span). [span_20](start_span)No additional nutrients are added after the start, and nothing is removed until the end of the process, except for air and gases[span_20](end_span).

(ii) Phases of growth:

[span_21](start_span)

Microbial growth in a batch system follows four distinct phases[span_21](end_span):

    [span_22](start_span)
  1. Lag Phase: Cells adapt to the new environment; no increase in number[span_22](end_span).
    2. [span_23](start_span)
  2. Log (Exponential) Phase: Cells divide at a constant, maximum rate[span_23](end_span).
    3. [span_24](start_span)
  3. Stationary Phase: Growth rate equals death rate due to nutrient depletion or waste accumulation[span_24](end_span).
    4. [span_25](start_span)
  4. Death (Decline) Phase: Cells die exponentially as conditions become toxic[span_25](end_span).

Question 6(a): Give a detailed account of sterilization. 10 Marks

[span_50](start_span)

Sterilization is the total destruction of all living organisms and spores to ensure monosepsis in the fermentation vessel[span_50](end_span).

    [span_51](start_span)
  • Batch Sterilization: The medium is heated in the bioreactor itself (typically 121°C for 20-30 mins)[span_51](end_span).
    • [span_52](start_span)
  • Continuous Sterilization: The medium is passed through a heat exchanger, reaching high temperatures (140°C) for very short durations (seconds), which minimizes nutrient degradation[span_52](end_span).
    • [span_53](start_span)
  • Filter Sterilization: Used for heat-sensitive components; medium is passed through membranes with 0.22 μm pores to physically remove microbes[span_53](end_span).
