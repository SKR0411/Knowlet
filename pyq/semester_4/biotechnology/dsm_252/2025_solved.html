Question 4(a): Write notes on: (i) Growth curve in microbes (ii) Generation time in bacteria 5+5=10 Marks

(i) Growth curve in microbes:

[span_23](start_span)

When bacteria are grown in a closed system, they follow a predictable pattern consisting of the Lag phase (adaptation), Log/Exponential phase (rapid division), Stationary phase (growth = death), and Death phase (nutrient depletion)[span_23](end_span).

(ii) Generation time:

[span_24](start_span)

The time required for a bacterial population to double in number during the log phase is called generation time (or doubling time)[span_24](end_span). [span_25](start_span)It varies significantly depending on the species and environmental conditions[span_25](end_span).

Question 4(b): Give a detailed account of bacterial conjugation and transformation. 5+5=10 Marks

Bacterial Conjugation:

[span_26](start_span)

Conjugation is the direct transfer of DNA between two bacterial cells that are in physical contact, typically mediated by a sex pilus and a fertility (F) plasmid[span_26](end_span).

Bacterial Transformation:

[span_27](start_span)

Transformation is the process by which a competent bacterial cell takes up "naked" DNA fragments from its surrounding environment and incorporates them into its own genome[span_27](end_span).

Immunoglobulin (Antibody) Structure:

Antibodies are Y-shaped proteins consisting of four polypeptide chains: two identical heavy chains and two identical light chains, held together by disulfide bonds[span_44](end_span). [span_47](start_span)

Autoimmune diseases result from a failure of the immune system to distinguish between "self" and "non-self," leading to the immune system attacking the body's own tissues and organs[span_47](end_span).

MHC (Major Histocompatibility Complex) molecules are cell surface proteins that display antigen fragments to T-cells. [span_48](start_span)MHC Class I is found on all nucleated cells, while MHC Class II is found on specialized antigen-presenting cells[span_48](end_span).

[span_49](start_span)
