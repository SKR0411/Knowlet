Question 2(a) [Option A] 10 Marks

Discuss the political conditions of Europe in the 13th century.

[span_5](start_span)

The 13th century in Europe was a period of significant political transition characterized by the following features[span_5](end_span):

    [span_6](start_span)
  • Feudal Fragmentation: Political power was largely decentralized among various feudal lords, though the century saw the gradual rise of centralized monarchies in regions like France and England[span_6](end_span).
    • [span_7](start_span)
  • Power of the Papacy: The Catholic Church, particularly under Pope Innocent III, reached the zenith of its political influence, often asserting authority over secular monarchs[span_7](end_span).
    • [span_8](start_span)
  • The Holy Roman Empire: The Empire faced internal struggles for power and conflicts with the Papacy, leading to a decline in centralized imperial authority[span_8](end_span).
    • [span_9](start_span)
  • Rise of Parliamentary Institutions: Significant developments occurred, such as the signing of the Magna Carta (1215) in England, which began to limit the absolute power of kings[span_9](end_span).
    • [span_10](start_span)
  • Expansion and Conflict: This era was marked by the later Crusades and the beginning of the Reconquista in the Iberian Peninsula[span_10](end_span).

Question 2(b) [Option B] 10 Marks

What were the main causes of the Fall of Constantinople and why was it significant?

[span_11](start_span)

The Fall of Constantinople in 1453 to the Ottoman Turks was a watershed event in world history[span_11](end_span).

Causes:

    [span_12](start_span)
  • Military Superiority: Sultan Mehmed II utilized advanced gunpowder technology, including massive cannons, to breach the legendary Theodosian Walls[span_12](end_span).
    • [span_13](start_span)
  • Isolation: The Byzantine Empire had shrunk to just the city itself and was largely ignored by Western European powers who were preoccupied with internal conflicts[span_13](end_span).

Significance:

    [span_14](start_span)
  • End of the Byzantine Empire: It marked the final collapse of the Roman Empire's eastern successor[span_14](end_span).
    • [span_15](start_span)
  • Spread of the Renaissance: Greek scholars fled to Italy carrying ancient manuscripts, which directly fueled the Renaissance[span_15](end_span).
    • [span_16](start_span)
  • Age of Discovery: The Ottoman control of silk road trade routes forced Europeans to find new sea routes to the East, leading to the discovery of the Americas[span_16](end_span).

What were the key causes and significant effects of the reformation in Europe?

Causes:

    [span_19](start_span)

Effects:

    • [span_23](start_span)

    • [span_24](start_span)
  • Counter-Reformation: The Catholic Church underwent its own internal reforms (Council of Trent) to address criticisms[span_24](end_span).

Question 6(b) [Option B] 10 Marks

How effectively did Louis XIV embody the principles of absolutism as the ruler of France?

[span_27](start_span)

Louis XIV, known as the "Sun King," is considered the ultimate example of an absolute monarch[span_27](end_span).

    [span_28](start_span)
  • Divine Right Theory: He firmly believed his authority came directly from God, famously stating "L'état, c'est moi" (I am the state)[span_28](end_span).
    • [span_29](start_span)
  • Palace of Versailles: He used the grand palace to control the nobility, requiring them to live there and participate in elaborate court rituals, thereby preventing them from plotting against him[span_29](end_span).
    • [span_30](start_span)
  • Centralized Administration: He weakened the power of traditional nobles by using middle-class "intendants" to collect taxes and administer justice directly for the crown[span_30](end_span).
    • [span_31](start_span)
  • Religious Uniformity: By revoking the Edict of Nantes, he enforced Catholicism across France to ensure political and social unity[span_31](end_span).

What is feudalism? State two features.

[span_32](start_span)
Feudalism was a social and political system in medieval Europe based on land ownership and personal loyalty[span_32](end_span).

Features:

    [span_33](start_span)

    • [span_34](start_span)

Question 8(a) [Option A] 10 Marks

Analyze the causes behind the Industrial Revolution in Europe.

[span_35](start_span)

The Industrial Revolution began in Britain due to a unique combination of factors[span_35](end_span):

    [span_36](start_span)
  • Agricultural Revolution: Improved farming techniques increased food supply and forced displaced peasants to seek work in factories[span_36](end_span).
    • [span_37](start_span)
  • Natural Resources: Abundant supplies of coal and iron ore were essential for powering steam engines and building machinery[span_37](end_span).
    • [span_38](start_span)
  • Capital and Banking: Britain had a stable banking system and wealth from overseas trade to invest in new industries[span_38](end_span).
    • [span_39](start_span)
  • Technological Innovations: Inventions like the Spinning Jenny, Water Frame, and the Steam Engine revolutionized production[span_39](end_span).
    • [span_40](start_span)
  • Transport Network: A vast system of canals and later railways allowed for the cheap transport of raw materials and finished goods[span_40](end_span).

Question 10(a) [Option A] 10 Marks

Write briefly about the Glorious Revolution in England.

[span_42](start_span)

The Glorious Revolution (1688) was "glorious" because it achieved a major political change with almost no bloodshed[span_42](end_span).

    [span_43](start_span)
  • The Conflict: King James II's pro-Catholic policies and absolute tendencies alienated the Protestant majority and Parliament[span_43](end_span).
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • The Invitation: Seven prominent English nobles invited William of Orange and his wife Mary (James's daughter) to invade England and take the throne[span_44](end_span).
    • [span_45](start_span)
  • The Result: James II fled to France, and William and Mary were declared joint monarchs after agreeing to the Bill of Rights (1689)[span_45](end_span).
    • [span_46](start_span)
  • Significance: It permanently shifted power from the monarchy to Parliament, establishing a Constitutional Monarchy in England[span_46](end_span).
