(a) Define mutually exclusive events.[span_1](end_span)

Two events are said to be mutually exclusive if they cannot occur at the same time. [span_2](start_span)If event A happens, event B cannot happen, meaning their intersection is an empty set (P(A ∩ B) = 0).[span_2](end_span)

[span_3](start_span)

(b) State the classical definition of probability.[span_3](end_span)

[span_4](start_span)If a random experiment has 'n' mutually exclusive, equally likely, and exhaustive outcomes, and 'm' of these are favorable to an event A, then the probability of A is P(A) = m / n.[span_4](end_span)
[span_5](start_span)

(c) State the multiplication law of probability.[span_5](end_span)

[span_6](start_span)

For two independent events A and B, the probability of both occurring is the product of their individual probabilities: P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B).[span_6](end_span)

(ii) Define conditional probability.[span_11](end_span)

[span_12](start_span)[span_13](start_span)

Conditional probability is the probability of an event A occurring given that event B has already occurred, denoted as P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B), where P(B) > 0.[span_12](end_span)[span_13](end_span)

[span_14](start_span)

(iii) Probability that a leap year contains 53 Mondays.[span_14](end_span)

[span_15](start_span)[span_16](start_span)

A leap year has 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 extra days.[span_15](end_span)[span_16](end_span)
Possible pairs for extra days: (Sun, Mon), (Mon, Tue), (Tue, Wed), (Wed, Thu), (Thu, Fri), (Fri, Sat), (Sat, Sun). Total = 7.
Favorable pairs (containing Monday): (Sun, Mon) and (Mon, Tue). Favorable = 2.
