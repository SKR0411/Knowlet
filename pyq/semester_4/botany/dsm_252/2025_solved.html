Question 2(a): Discuss the mechanism of opening and closing of stomata with suitable model. 10 Marks

[span_10](start_span)

The most widely accepted model is the K+ Ion Transport Mechanism (Levitt's Model)[span_10](end_span).

Stomatal Opening (Daytime):

    [span_11](start_span)
  • Photosynthesis occurs in guard cells, leading to a decrease in CO2 concentration[span_11](end_span).
    • [span_12](start_span)
  • Malic acid is formed, which dissociates into malate ions and protons (H+)[span_12](end_span).
    • [span_13](start_span)
  • Protons are actively pumped out of guard cells, and Potassium ions (K+) are actively taken in from subsidiary cells[span_13](end_span).
    • [span_14](start_span)
  • This increases the osmotic concentration of guard cells, causing water to enter (endosmosis)[span_14](end_span).
    • [span_15](start_span)
  • Guard cells become turgid, their thin outer walls stretch, and the thick inner walls pull apart, opening the pore[span_15](end_span).

Stomatal Closing (Nighttime):

    [span_16](start_span)
  • Photosynthesis stops, CO2 increases, and the plant hormone Abscisic Acid (ABA) may be produced[span_16](end_span).
    • [span_17](start_span)
  • K+ ions leak out of the guard cells back into subsidiary cells[span_17](end_span).
    • [span_18](start_span)
  • Osmotic potential decreases, water leaves the guard cells (exosmosis), and they become flaccid, closing the pore[span_18](end_span).

Question 2(b): Discuss the mechanism of transport of water by plants. 10 Marks

[span_19](start_span)

The transport of water from roots to leaves (ascent of sap) is explained by the Cohesion-Tension Theory[span_19](end_span).

    [span_20](start_span)
  • Transpiration Pull: Evaporation of water from leaves creates a negative pressure (tension) in the xylem[span_20](end_span).
    • [span_21](start_span)
  • Cohesion: Water molecules stick to each other due to hydrogen bonding, forming a continuous water column[span_21](end_span).
    • [span_22](start_span)
  • Adhesion: Water molecules stick to the walls of xylem vessels[span_22](end_span).
    • [span_23](start_span)
  • Root Pressure: Active transport of minerals into roots creates an osmotic gradient that pushes water upward to a limited height[span_23](end_span).

Question 4(a): With the help of suitable diagram, describe the mechanism of phloem loading and unloading in plants. 10 Marks

[span_31](start_span)

The movement of food in phloem occurs via the Pressure Flow Hypothesis (Munch's hypothesis)[span_31](end_span).

    [span_32](start_span)
  • Phloem Loading: At the source (leaf), sucrose is actively transported into the sieve tubes[span_32](end_span). [span_33](start_span)This lowers the water potential, causing water to enter from the xylem, creating high turgor pressure[span_33](end_span).
    • [span_34](start_span)
  • Translocation: The mass flow of sap moves from high pressure (source) to low pressure (sink)[span_34](end_span).
    • [span_35](start_span)
  • Phloem Unloading: At the sink (root/fruit), sucrose is actively or passively removed from the sieve tubes[span_35](end_span). [span_36](start_span)Water then follows out back to the xylem, reducing turgor pressure[span_36](end_span).
