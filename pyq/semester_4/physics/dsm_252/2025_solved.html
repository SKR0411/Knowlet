(a) State and explain Gauss's law of electrostatics.[span_0](end_span)

[span_1](start_span)

Answer: Gauss's law states that the total electric flux through any closed surface is equal to 1/ε₀ times the net charge enclosed by that surface.[span_1](end_span) Mathematically:

Φ = ∮ E · dA = q_enclosed / ε₀
[span_2](start_span)This law relates the distribution of electric charge to the resulting electric field.[span_2](end_span)

(b) Define electric field and electric flux.

Answer:

    [span_3](start_span)
  • Electric Field: It is defined as the force experienced per unit positive test charge placed at a point in space.[span_3](end_span)
    • [span_4](start_span)
  • Electric Flux: It is the measure of the total number of electric lines of force passing normally through a given area.[span_4](end_span)

(c) Write down the properties of electric lines of force.

Answer:

    [span_5](start_span)
  • They start from a positive charge and end on a negative charge.[span_5](end_span)
    • [span_6](start_span)
  • They never intersect each other.[span_6](end_span)
    • [span_7](start_span)
  • The tangent to a line of force at any point gives the direction of the electric field at that point.[span_7](end_span)
    • [span_8](start_span)

2. (a) Electric Field of Spherical Shell

[span_9](start_span)

Define electric charge (SI unit) and derive the expression of electric field due to a uniformly charged spherical shell at points inside and outside.[span_9](end_span)

Answer: [span_10](start_span)Electric Charge: It is a fundamental property of matter that causes it to experience a force when placed in an electromagnetic field.[span_10](end_span) [span_11](start_span)The SI unit is the **Coulomb (C)**.[span_11](end_span)

Derivation: Consider a spherical shell of radius R with charge Q. 1. Outside the shell (r > R): Construct a Gaussian sphere of radius r. By Gauss's Law: E(4πr²) = Q/ε₀ ⇒

E = Q / (4πε₀r²)
2. Inside the shell (r < R): Since the charge resides on the surface, q_enclosed = 0. By Gauss's Law: E(4πr²) = 0 ⇒ [span_12](start_span)
E = 0
