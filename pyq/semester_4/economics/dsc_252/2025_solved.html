2. (a) Show graphically how equilibrium level of national income is determined in the AD-AS model. 10

[span_15](start_span)

In the AD-AS model, the equilibrium level of national income and price level is determined at the intersection of the Aggregate Demand (AD) and Aggregate Supply (AS) curves.[span_15](end_span)

    [span_16](start_span)
  • Aggregate Demand (AD): Represents the total spending on domestic goods at different price levels.[span_16](end_span)
    • [span_17](start_span)
  • Aggregate Supply (AS): Represents the total production of goods at different price levels.[span_17](end_span)
  • Equilibrium: Point where AD = AS. [span_18](start_span)If price is above equilibrium, there is a surplus; if below, there is a shortage.[span_18](end_span)

2. (b) [span_19](start_span)Explain the derivation of IS-LM curves and how they interact to determine the equilibrium in the goods and money markets.[span_19](end_span) 10

Derivation:

  • IS Curve: Derived from the Keynesian Cross. [span_20](start_span)It represents all combinations of interest rates (r) and income levels (Y) where the goods market is in equilibrium (I = S).[span_20](end_span)
  • LM Curve: Derived from the Theory of Liquidity Preference. [span_21](start_span)It represents combinations where money demand (L) equals money supply (M).[span_21](end_span)
[span_22](start_span)

Interaction: The intersection of the IS and LM curves determines the unique pair of interest rate and income level that simultaneously clears both the goods and money markets.[span_22](end_span)

4. (b) [span_38](start_span)Critically examine the psychological law of consumption as proposed by Keynes.[span_38](end_span) 10

[span_39](start_span)

The Law: Keynes stated that men are disposed to increase their consumption as their income increases, but not by as much as the increase in their income.[span_39](end_span)

Critical Examination:

    [span_40](start_span)
  • Assumptions: Constant institutional and psychological factors, and a normal environment.[span_40](end_span)
    • [span_41](start_span)
  • Implications: Leads to a declining APC and necessitates investment to fill the gap between income and consumption.[span_41](end_span)
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • Criticism: It is a short-run observation; in the long run, APC tends to remain constant (Kuznets' findings).[span_42](end_span)
