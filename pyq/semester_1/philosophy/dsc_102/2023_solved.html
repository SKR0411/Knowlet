Q18. What, according to Plato is the ultimate reality? Can the 'Ideas' of Plato be known by experience? [span_52](start_span)Explain Plato's theory of Ideas.[span_52](end_span)

Ultimate Reality:

For Plato, the ultimate reality is the World of Ideas (or Forms). [span_53](start_span)These are eternal, unchanging, and perfect archetypes of all things[span_53](end_span).

Knowledge by Experience:

No, Ideas cannot be known by sense experience. Experience only provides "opinion" of the shadows of reality. [span_54](start_span)Ideas are known only through Reason[span_54](end_span).

Theory of Ideas (Forms):

Plato argues that every object in the physical world is a mere imperfect copy of its perfect Form. [span_55](start_span)For example, all beautiful things are beautiful only because they participate in the "Idea of Beauty"[span_55](end_span).

Hume reduces causation to Constant Conjunction[span_90](end_span). Because we always see event A followed by event B, our mind develops a "habit" or "custom" of expecting B when A occurs.
