Q18. Why does Carvaka reject the existence of God? Discuss Carvaka critique of inference.

Part A: Rejection of God

[span_40](start_span)

Carvaka rejects God because God cannot be perceived.[span_40](end_span) According to their epistemology, Pratyaksa (perception) is the only valid source of knowledge. [span_41](start_span)Since God is neither visible nor detectable through any sense organ, God's existence is a myth created by priests for their own benefit.[span_41](end_span)

Part B: Critique of Inference (Anumana)

[span_42](start_span)

Carvaka argues that inference is not a valid Pramana for the following reasons:[span_42](end_span)

  • Lack of Universal Relation (Vyapti): Inference depends on Vyapti (smoke implies fire). [span_43](start_span)Carvaka asks: How do we know this relation is universal?[span_43](end_span)
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • Perception is Limited: We can perceive individual cases of smoke and fire, but we cannot perceive all cases in all times and places.[span_44](end_span)
    • [span_45](start_span)
  • Circular Reasoning: If we use another inference to prove Vyapti, it leads to an infinite regress (Anavastha).[span_45](end_span)
[span_46](start_span)
Conclusion: For Carvaka, inference provides only a probability, not certain knowledge.[span_46](end_span)

Q19. Explain and examine Jaina theory of Syadvada.

[span_47](start_span)

Syadvada is the Jaina theory of the relativity of knowledge.[span_47](end_span) [span_48](start_span)It suggests that all judgements are true only under certain conditions or from a particular standpoint (Naya).[span_48](end_span)

The Seven-fold Judgement (Saptabhangi Naya):

  1. Syad Asti: Relatively, a thing exists.
  2. Syad Nasti: Relatively, a thing does not exist.
  3. Syad Asti Nasti: Relatively, it exists and does not exist.
  4. Syad Avaktavyam: Relatively, it is indescribable.
  5. Syad Asti ca Avaktavyam: Relatively, it exists and is indescribable.
  6. Syad Nasti ca Avaktavyam: Relatively, it does not exist and is indescribable.
  7. Syad Asti Nasti ca Avaktavyam: Relatively, it exists, does not exist, and is indescribable.
[span_49](start_span)

Critical Examination: While Syadvada promotes intellectual tolerance (Anekantavada), critics like Sankara argue it leads to skepticism and contradiction.[span_49](end_span)

Q20. Is there any permanent Self according to Buddha? Explain Niratmavada as a theory of Self according to Buddha.

Part A: Permanent Self

[span_50](start_span)

No, Buddha explicitly rejected the existence of a permanent, eternal, and unchanging soul (Atman).[span_50](end_span)

Part B: Niratmavada (Anatta)

[span_51](start_span)

Niratmavada is the doctrine of "No-Self."[span_51](end_span) [span_52](start_span)Buddha argued that what we call the "Self" is actually a collection of five changing aggregates (Skandhas):[span_52](end_span)

  • Rupa: Matter/Body
  • Vedana: Feelings/Sensation
  • Samjna: Perception
  • Samskara: Mental dispositions
  • Vijnana: Consciousness
[span_53](start_span)

Just as a chariot is a name for a collection of parts (wheels, axle, etc.), the "Self" is a conventional name for these ever-changing processes.[span_53](end_span)

Q24. What is Purusa? How does Samkhya prove the existence of Purusa? Is Purusa one or many?

Definition:

[span_69](start_span)

Purusa is the pure consciousness, eternal, and inactive witness in Samkhya philosophy.[span_69](end_span)

Proofs for the existence of Purusa:

    [span_70](start_span)
  • Teleological Argument: All material objects (Prakrti) exist for the sake of someone else (Purusa).[span_70](end_span)
    • [span_71](start_span)
  • Control Argument: Matter must be controlled by a conscious agent.[span_71](end_span)
    • [span_72](start_span)
  • Enjoyment Argument: There must be an enjoyer of the modifications of Prakrti.[span_72](end_span)
    • [span_73](start_span)
  • Aspiration for Liberation: The desire to be free implies a conscious being who can be free.[span_73](end_span)

Plurality of Purusa:

[span_74](start_span)

Samkhya believes that Purusa is Many (Plurality of Selves) because different individuals have different births, deaths, and experiences.[span_74](end_span)

Q25. Explain critically Advaita doctrine of Maya.

[span_75](start_span)

In Advaita Vedanta, Maya is the power of Brahman that makes the one appear as many.[span_75](end_span)

Characteristics:

    [span_76](start_span)
  • Anirvacaniya: It is neither real (Sat) nor unreal (Asat); it is indescribable.[span_76](end_span)
    • [span_77](start_span)
  • Beginningless (Anadi): It has no start, but it ends with the realization of Brahman.[span_77](end_span)
    • [span_78](start_span)
  • Subjective and Objective: It conceals reality and projects the world.[span_78](end_span)

Critical View: Critics (like Ramanuja) ask: Where does Maya reside? If it resides in Brahman, Brahman's purity is compromised. [span_79](start_span)If in Jiva, Jiva itself is a product of Maya.[span_79](end_span)
