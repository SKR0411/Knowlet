32. Discuss the relationship between archaeology and history.

[span_25](start_span)

Archaeology and History are complementary disciplines that work together to reconstruct the human past[span_25](end_span).

    [span_26](start_span)
  • Archaeology provides primary material evidence (artifacts, structures) that can verify or challenge written historical records[span_26](end_span).
    • [span_27](start_span)
  • For prehistoric periods where written records do not exist, archaeology is the sole source of historical information[span_27](end_span).
    • [span_28](start_span)
  • History provides the cultural and chronological context that helps archaeologists interpret their findings[span_28](end_span).
    • [span_29](start_span)
  • Together, they offer a more complete and accurate picture of civilizations than either could provide alone[span_29](end_span).

5

34. Assess the contribution of Sir John Marshall in Indian archaeology.

[span_30](start_span)

Sir John Marshall, as the Director General of ASI (1902–1928), revolutionized Indian archaeology[span_30](end_span).

    [span_31](start_span)
  • He oversaw the landmark excavations of Harappa and Mohenjo-daro, pushing back the date of Indian civilization by thousands of years[span_31](end_span).
    • [span_32](start_span)
  • He was instrumental in the excavation and conservation of sites like Taxila, Sanchi, and Sarnath[span_32](end_span).
    • [span_33](start_span)
  • Marshall introduced modern scientific methods to Indian archaeology and focused on the conservation of standing monuments[span_33](end_span).
    • [span_34](start_span)
  • He established several site museums, ensuring that artifacts remained close to their original historical context[span_34](end_span).

5

35. Assess the contribution of R. D. Banerji in the field of archaeology.

[span_35](start_span)

Rakhaldas Bandyopadhyay (R. D. Banerji) was a pioneering Indian archaeologist whose most significant contribution was the discovery of Mohenjo-daro in 1922[span_35](end_span).

    [span_36](start_span)
  • While searching for Buddhist remains, he recognized the prehistoric significance of the mound at Mohenjo-daro, identifying it as a site much older than previously thought[span_36](end_span).
    • [span_37](start_span)
  • His discovery provided the first tangible evidence of the Indus Valley Civilization's antiquity[span_37](end_span).
    • [span_38](start_span)
  • Beyond Mohenjo-daro, he conducted extensive research on Indian epigraphy, numismatics, and medieval history[span_38](end_span).
    • [span_39](start_span)
  • His work helped lay the groundwork for an indigenous school of archaeological thought in India[span_39](end_span).

5

37. Give a brief description of the Sun Temple of Konark.

[span_40](start_span)

The Sun Temple of Konark, built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I, is a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture[span_40](end_span).

    [span_41](start_span)
  • The temple is designed as a colossal chariot for the Sun God (Surya), complete with 12 pairs of exquisitely carved stone wheels and pulled by seven stone horses[span_41](end_span).
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • It is renowned for its intricate carvings, depicting everything from daily life and mythological scenes to erotic figures[span_42](end_span).
    • [span_43](start_span)
  • The structure was built using Khondalite rocks and was originally much taller before parts of the main sanctum collapsed[span_43](end_span).
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and remains one of India's most iconic archaeological landmarks[span_44](end_span).

[span_45](start_span)

The Indian Museum in Kolkata, founded in 1814, is the oldest and largest museum in India[span_45](end_span).

    [span_46](start_span)
  • It was established by the Asiatic Society of Bengal and is often referred to as "Jadu Ghar" (House of Magic) by locals[span_46](end_span).
    • [span_47](start_span)
  • The museum houses a massive collection across various sections: Archaeology, Anthropology, Geology, Zoology, and Botany[span_47](end_span).
    • [span_48](start_span)
  • Its archaeological gallery is famous for the Bharhut Stupa railings and a rich collection of Gandhara and Mathura art[span_48](end_span).
    • [span_49](start_span)
  • It serves as a central institution for research and preservation of India's cultural and natural heritage[span_49](end_span).
