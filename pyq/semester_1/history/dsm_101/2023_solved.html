UNIT-I

16. [span_35](start_span)Write a note on the sources of ancient Indian history.[span_35](end_span)

[span_36](start_span)

The reconstruction of ancient Indian history is based on two main categories of sources: Literary and Archaeological.[span_36](end_span)

    [span_37](start_span)
  • Literary Sources: Includes religious texts like the Vedas, Upanishads, and Puranas, as well as secular works like the Arthashastra and biographies (Harshacharita).[span_37](end_span) [span_38](start_span)Foreign accounts by Megasthenes and Chinese travelers are also vital.[span_38](end_span)
    • [span_39](start_span)
  • Archaeological Sources: These provide more reliable dates and include inscriptions (e.g., Ashokan Edicts), numismatics (coins), and monuments.[span_39](end_span) [span_40](start_span)Excavations at sites like Mohenjo-Daro offer direct evidence of material culture.[span_40](end_span)

UNIT-II

10 Marks

18. [span_41](start_span)Give a description of the town planning system of the Indus Valley Civilization.[span_41](end_span)

[span_42](start_span)

The town planning of the Indus Valley Civilization remains its most impressive feature, showcasing advanced engineering skills.[span_42](end_span)

    [span_43](start_span)
  • Grid Pattern: Streets were laid out in a straight grid pattern, intersecting at right angles, dividing the city into blocks.[span_43](end_span)
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • Citadel and Lower Town: Most cities were divided into a fortified Citadel (upper part) for administrative use and a Lower Town for common citizens.[span_44](end_span)
    • [span_45](start_span)
  • Drainage System: An elaborate covered drainage system connected every house, reflecting a high priority for hygiene and public health.[span_45](end_span)
    • [span_46](start_span)
  • Standardized Bricks: Houses were built with burnt bricks of uniform size, a technology ahead of its time.[span_46](end_span)

20. [span_47](start_span)Draw a picture of the socio-economic life of the Rig-Vedic people.[span_47](end_span)

[span_48](start_span)

The Rig-Vedic period (c. 1500–1000 BCE) was characterized by a tribal structure and pastoral economy.[span_48](end_span)

    [span_49](start_span)
  • Social Life: Society was organized into tribes (Janas).[span_49](end_span) [span_50](start_span)The family (Kula) was the basic unit, and patriarchal authority was central.[span_50](end_span) [span_51](start_span)Women held a respectable position and could participate in tribal assemblies.[span_51](end_span)
    • [span_52](start_span)
  • Economic Life: Cattle rearing was the mainstay, with the cow being the most valued possession (wealth).[span_52](end_span) [span_53](start_span)Agriculture was secondary, and the barter system was the primary mode of exchange.[span_53](end_span)

10 Marks

22. [span_54](start_span)Give an idea of the political condition of India in the Sixth Century BC.[span_54](end_span)

[span_55](start_span)

The 6th century BC was a period of "Second Urbanization" and significant political transition.[span_55](end_span)

    [span_56](start_span)
  • Mahajanapadas: The landscape was dominated by sixteen great states known as Mahajanapadas.[span_56](end_span)
    • [span_57](start_span)
  • Monarchies and Republics: States like Magadha and Kosala were monarchies, while others like the Vrijjis were Gana-Sanghas (republican or oligarchic).[span_57](end_span)
    • [span_58](start_span)
  • Rise of Magadha: Due to strategic advantages (iron ore, fertile land), Magadha began its rise to dominance over other states.[span_58](end_span)

UNIT-V

10 Marks

24. [span_59](start_span)Make an estimate of the Mauryan administration.[span_59](end_span)

[span_60](start_span)

The Mauryas established India's first highly centralized and bureaucratic empire.[span_60](end_span)

    [span_61](start_span)
  • Central Administration: The King was the supreme head, assisted by a council of ministers (Mantriparishad).[span_61](end_span)
    • [span_62](start_span)
  • Bureaucracy: A complex network of officers (Adhyakshas) managed departments like trade, agriculture, and military.[span_62](end_span)
    • [span_63](start_span)
  • Provincial Setup: The empire was divided into provinces, often governed by royal princes (Kumaras).[span_63](end_span)
    • [span_64](start_span)
  • Espionage: A well-organized spy system (Gudha-purushas) provided the king with real-time intelligence.[span_64](end_span)
