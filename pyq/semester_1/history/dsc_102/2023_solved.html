Question 1: Short Answers

(a) Who was Al-Beruni? Name one of his famous books. [2]

[span_7](start_span)

Al-Beruni was a Persian scholar and polymath who accompanied Mahmud of Ghazni to India[span_7](end_span). [span_8](start_span)His most famous book is Kitab-ul-Hind (or Tahqiq-i-Hind), which provides a detailed account of Indian religion, philosophy, and social customs[span_8](end_span).

[span_9](start_span)

Zia-uddin Barani was a 14th-century Muslim political thinker and historian during the period of the Delhi Sultanate[span_9](end_span). [span_10](start_span)He is best known for his work Tarikh-i-Firoz Shahi, which covers the history from Balban to the early years of Firoz Shah Tughluq[span_10](end_span).

Two primary literary sources are Tabaqat-i-Nasiri by Minhaj-us-Siraj and Tarikh-i-Firoz Shahi by Zia-uddin Barani[span_11](end_span).

Question 2: Descriptive (Select One)

(a) Discuss the various sources of Medieval Indian History (1206 to 1526 CE). [10]

[span_12](start_span)

The history of the Delhi Sultanate is reconstructed through a diverse range of sources[span_12](end_span):

    [span_13](start_span)
  • Literary Sources (Tawarikh): Written mostly in Persian, these include court chronicles like Minhaj-us-Siraj's Tabaqat-i-Nasiri and Barani's Tarikh-i-Firoz Shahi[span_13](end_span).
  • Travelogues: Foreign travelers provided independent perspectives. [span_14](start_span)Notable examples include Ibn Battuta (Rihla) and Marco Polo[span_14](end_span).
    • [span_15](start_span)
  • Archaeological Sources: Monuments like the Qutub Minar and Alai Darwaza reveal architectural styles and the grandeur of the era[span_15](end_span).
    • [span_16](start_span)
  • Epigraphy and Numismatics: Inscriptions on buildings and coins minted by Sultans like Iltutmish and Muhammad Bin Tughluq help establish chronology and economic conditions[span_16](end_span).

(b) Provide an overview of the career and key achievements of Qutubuddin Aibak. [10]

[span_17](start_span)

Qutubuddin Aibak was the founder of the Slave Dynasty and the Delhi Sultanate[span_17](end_span).

    [span_18](start_span)
  • Early Life: Originally a slave of Muhammad Ghori, he rose to prominence due to his military skill[span_18](end_span).
    • [span_19](start_span)[span_20](start_span)
  • Foundation of Rule: In 1206, he became the first independent Muslim ruler in India[span_19](end_span)[span_20](end_span).
    • [span_21](start_span)
  • Military Success: He consolidated Turkish rule in Northern India, capturing key territories[span_21](end_span).
    • [span_22](start_span)
  • Architecture: He initiated the construction of the Qutub Minar and built the Quwwat-ul-Islam mosque in Delhi[span_22](end_span).
    • [span_23](start_span)
  • Generosity: Known as 'Lakh Baksh' (Giver of Lakhs) for his charitable nature[span_23](end_span).

Question 4: Descriptive (Select One)

(a) Write a brief note on the conquests of Alauddin Khilji. [10]

[span_28](start_span)

Alauddin Khilji was the first Sultan to expand the Sultanate into Southern India[span_28](end_span).

    [span_29](start_span)[span_30](start_span)
  • Northern Conquests: He conquered Gujarat, Ranthambore, Chittor, and Malwa[span_29](end_span)[span_30](end_span).
    • [span_31](start_span)
  • Southern Campaigns: Under his general Malik Kafur, he defeated the Yadavas of Devagiri, Kakatiyas of Warangal, and Hoysalas of Dwarasamudra[span_31](end_span).
    • [span_32](start_span)
  • Objective: Unlike the North, his Southern policy was aimed at extracting wealth and ensuring vassalage rather than direct annexation[span_32](end_span).

Question 6: Descriptive (Select One)

(b) Analyze the key causes and significant consequences of the 1st Battle of Panipat. [10]

[span_38](start_span)

The First Battle of Panipat (1526) was a turning point in Indian history[span_38](end_span).

    [span_39](start_span)
  • Causes: The invitation to Babur by Daulat Khan Lodi and the unpopularity/instability of Ibrahim Lodi's rule[span_39](end_span).
    • [span_40](start_span)
  • Babur's Strategy: Use of gunpowder, artillery, and the Tulghuma (flanking) tactic[span_40](end_span).
    • [span_41](start_span)
  • Consequences: The death of Ibrahim Lodi, the end of the Delhi Sultanate, and the foundation of the Mughal Empire in India[span_41](end_span).
