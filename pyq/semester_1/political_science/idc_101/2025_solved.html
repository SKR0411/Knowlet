9. (a) Critically discuss the classical theory of full employment. 8
[span_32](start_span)

The Classical theory assumes that the economy always operates at full employment in the long run due to flexible wages and prices.[span_32](end_span)

    [span_33](start_span)
  • Say's Law: Overproduction is impossible because supply generates its own demand.[span_33](end_span)
    • [span_34](start_span)
  • Wage-Price Flexibility: If unemployment exists, wages will fall until everyone is employed.[span_34](end_span)
    • [span_35](start_span)
  • Critique: Keynes criticized this during the Great Depression, arguing that wages are "sticky" downwards and that demand, not supply, determines employment levels.[span_35](end_span)
