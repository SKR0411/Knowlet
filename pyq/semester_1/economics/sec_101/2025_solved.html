1. [span_8](start_span)From which term 'entrepreneur' is derived?[span_8](end_span)

The term 'entrepreneur' is derived from the French verb 'entreprendre', which means "to undertake."

1 Mark

2. [span_9](start_span)Who is an entrepreneur?[span_9](end_span)

An entrepreneur is an individual who creates a new business, bearing most of the risks and enjoying most of the rewards.

1 Mark

3. [span_10](start_span)Mention any one barrier of entrepreneurship.[span_10](end_span)

[span_11](start_span)

Financial constraints: Lack of adequate capital or access to credit is a major barrier to starting a new venture.[span_11](end_span)

1 Mark

6. [span_12](start_span)What is project identification?[span_12](end_span)

[span_13](start_span)

Project identification is the process of identifying potential investment opportunities and screening them to select the most viable business project.[span_13](end_span)

8. [span_14](start_span)What is project report?[span_14](end_span)

[span_15](start_span)

1 Mark

9. [span_16](start_span)Define small-scale industry.[span_16](end_span)

31. [span_34](start_span)Discuss the important characteristics of entrepreneurship.[span_34](end_span)

    [span_35](start_span)
  • Economic Activity: It involves the creation and operation of an enterprise for profit.[span_35](end_span)
    • [span_36](start_span)
  • Innovation: A core trait where the entrepreneur introduces new combinations of factors of production.[span_36](end_span)
    • [span_37](start_span)
  • Risk Bearing: The entrepreneur assumes financial, social, and psychological risks.[span_37](end_span)
    • [span_38](start_span)
  • Purposeful Activity: It is directed toward achieving specific business objectives and growth.[span_38](end_span)
    • [span_39](start_span)
  • Organizing Function: It involves bringing together land, labor, and capital to produce goods or services.[span_39](end_span)

UNIT-III

5 Marks

36. [span_40](start_span)Discuss the role of small-scale industries in Indian economy.[span_40](end_span)

    [span_41](start_span)
  • Employment Generation: SSIs are labor-intensive and provide employment to a large number of people.[span_41](end_span)
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • Regional Development: They help in decentralizing industries and promoting balanced regional growth.[span_42](end_span)
    • [span_43](start_span)
  • Export Promotion: A significant portion of India's exports comes from the small-scale sector.[span_43](end_span)
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • Mobilization of Savings: They tap into local resources and small savings that might otherwise remain idle.[span_44](end_span)
    • [span_45](start_span)
  • Consumer Goods Production: They meet the local demand for a wide variety of consumer products.[span_45](end_span)

40. [span_46](start_span)Discuss the role of SHGs in the development of a country.[span_46](end_span)

    [span_47](start_span)
  • Women Empowerment: Self-Help Groups (SHGs) provide a platform for women to become financially independent.[span_47](end_span)
    • [span_48](start_span)
  • Financial Inclusion: They bridge the gap between rural poor and formal banking institutions.[span_48](end_span)
    • [span_49](start_span)
  • Skill Development: Members often receive training in various vocations, improving their employability.[span_49](end_span)
    • [span_50](start_span)
  • Poverty Alleviation: By providing small loans (microcredit), they help families start small businesses.[span_50](end_span)
    • [span_51](start_span)
  • Social Change: SHGs often tackle social issues like education, health, and sanitation in their communities.[span_51](end_span)

  • Interchanging the definitions of Microfinance (broad financial services) and Microcredit (specific small loans).
