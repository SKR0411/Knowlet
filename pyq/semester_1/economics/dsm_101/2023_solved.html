16. Distinguish between positive and normative economics. [span_45](start_span)Justify whether microeconomics is positive or normative in nature.[span_45](end_span)

[span_46](start_span)

Positive vs Normative Economics:[span_46](end_span)

  • Positive Economics: Deals with "what is." [span_47](start_span)It describes facts, cause-and-effect relationships, and objective data that can be tested (e.g., "An increase in tax leads to lower consumption").[span_47](end_span)
  • Normative Economics: Deals with "what ought to be." [span_48](start_span)It involves value judgments, ethics, and subjective opinions (e.g., "The government should provide free healthcare").[span_48](end_span)
[span_49](start_span)

Justification:[span_49](end_span)
Microeconomics is primarily positive in nature because it analyzes how individual agents (consumers/firms) behave and how prices are determined based on objective laws like the Law of Demand. [span_50](start_span)However, it also has normative elements, especially in "Welfare Economics," where it suggests how resources *should* be allocated to achieve maximum social welfare.[span_50](end_span)

18. State and explain the law of diminishing marginal utility. [span_59](start_span)Mention any two limitations of the law.[span_59](end_span)

[span_60](start_span)

Definition:[span_60](end_span)
[span_61](start_span)As a person consumes more and more units of a specific commodity, the utility derived from each successive unit goes on diminishing.[span_61](end_span)

[span_62](start_span)

Explanation:[span_62](end_span)
Total Utility (TU) increases at a decreasing rate while Marginal Utility (MU) falls. When TU is maximum, MU is zero. [span_63](start_span)When TU starts falling, MU becomes negative.[span_63](end_span)

[span_64](start_span)

Limitations:[span_64](end_span)

    [span_65](start_span)
  • Rationality: It assumes the consumer is rational and aims to maximize utility.[span_65](end_span)
    • [span_66](start_span)
  • Suitable Units: The units of the commodity must not be too small (e.g., drops of water vs a glass of water).[span_66](end_span)
