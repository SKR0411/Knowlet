[span_17](start_span)

12. [span_18](start_span)Define Average Product (AP) and Marginal Product (MP) of an input.[span_18](end_span)

    [span_19](start_span)
  • Average Product: Total output divided by the amount of variable input used (AP = TP / L).[span_19](end_span)
    • [span_20](start_span)
  • Marginal Product: The change in total output resulting from using one additional unit of variable input (MP = ΔTP / ΔL).[span_20](end_span)

40. Explain the law of variable proportions with the help of diagram. [span_39](start_span)Mention two limitations of the law.[span_39](end_span)

[span_40](start_span)

The Law of Variable Proportions (Short-run Production Function) explains how output changes when one variable input is increased while others are fixed.[span_40](end_span)

[span_41](start_span)

Stages:[span_41](end_span)

  1. Increasing Returns: TP increases at an increasing rate; [span_42](start_span)MP rises.[span_42](end_span)
  2. Diminishing Returns: TP increases at a decreasing rate; [span_43](start_span)MP falls but remains positive.[span_43](end_span)
  3. Negative Returns: TP starts falling; [span_44](start_span)MP becomes negative.[span_44](end_span)
[span_45](start_span)

Limitations:[span_45](end_span)

    [span_46](start_span)
  • It assumes technology remains constant.[span_46](end_span)
    • [span_47](start_span)
  • It assumes that at least some factors of production are fixed.[span_47](end_span)

44. Define Pareto optimality. [span_48](start_span)Explain the concept of Pareto optimality with the help of suitable diagram.[span_48](end_span)

[span_49](start_span)

Definition: Pareto Optimality (or Pareto Efficiency) is a state where resources are allocated in such a way that it is impossible to make any one individual better off without making at least one other individual worse off.[span_49](end_span)

Explanation: This concept is used to measure economic efficiency and social welfare. [span_50](start_span)In an Edge-worth Box diagram, Pareto optimal points are located on the contract curve where the marginal rates of substitution between two goods are equal for both consumers.[span_50](end_span)
