2. [span_11](start_span)What do you mean by 'nominal GDP' and 'real GDP'?[span_11](end_span)

    [span_12](start_span)

3. [span_14](start_span)Why is macroeconomics also known as 'theory of income and employment'?[span_14](end_span)

[span_15](start_span)

Macroeconomics is so-called because its primary focus is explaining the determination of the aggregate level of national income and the level of total employment in an economy.[span_15](end_span)

16. Define macroeconomics. [span_48](start_span)Briefly explain the nature and scope of macroeconomics.[span_48](end_span)

Definition:
[span_49](start_span)Macroeconomics is the branch of economics that deals with the performance, structure, behavior, and decision-making of an economy as a whole, rather than individual markets.[span_49](end_span)

Nature of Macroeconomics:

    [span_50](start_span)
  • Aggregate Study: It looks at totals like total consumption, total investment, and national income.[span_50](end_span)
    • [span_51](start_span)
  • Interdependence: It studies how different sectors (household, business, government) interact on a large scale.[span_51](end_span)

Scope of Macroeconomics:

    [span_52](start_span)
  • Theory of Income and Employment: Analysis of how national income and job levels are determined.[span_52](end_span)
    • [span_53](start_span)
  • Theory of General Price Level: Study of inflation and deflation.[span_53](end_span)
    • [span_54](start_span)
  • Theory of Economic Growth: Focusing on long-term increases in productive capacity.[span_54](end_span)
    • [span_55](start_span)
  • Macro Theory of Distribution: How total national income is shared among wages and profits.[span_55](end_span)

19. Elaborate the main functions of money. [span_56](start_span)In this connection, also discuss how money has overcome the difficulties of the barter system.[span_56](end_span)

Functions of Money:

    [span_57](start_span)
  • Primary: Medium of exchange and Measure of value.[span_57](end_span)
    • [span_58](start_span)
  • Secondary: Standard of deferred payments and Store of value.[span_58](end_span)
    • [span_59](start_span)
  • Contingent: Distribution of national income and basis of credit system.[span_59](end_span)

Overcoming Barter System Difficulties:

    [span_60](start_span)
  • Double Coincidence of Wants: Money eliminates the need for two people to want exactly what the other has.[span_60](end_span)
    • [span_61](start_span)
  • Common Measure of Value: Unlike barter, money provides a single price for all goods.[span_61](end_span)
    • [span_62](start_span)
  • Store of Value: Perishable goods in barter cannot be saved; money can be stored for future use.[span_62](end_span)
    • [span_63](start_span)
  • Divisibility: Money allows for small transactions which were difficult with large physical goods (like livestock).[span_63](end_span)

21. [span_64](start_span)Explain the role of effective demand in the Keynesian theory of income determination.[span_64](end_span)

[span_65](start_span)

In Keynesian economics, the level of national income and employment is determined by the level of Effective Demand.[span_65](end_span)

    [span_66](start_span)
  • Aggregate Demand Price (AD): The total amount of money that all sections of the economy are expected to spend on goods and services.[span_66](end_span)
    • [span_67](start_span)
  • Aggregate Supply Price (AS): The total cost of production that must be covered to provide a certain level of employment.[span_67](end_span)
  • Equilibrium: Employment is determined at the point where AD = AS. [span_68](start_span)This point is called Effective Demand.[span_68](end_span)
    • [span_69](start_span)
  • Key Conclusion: If effective demand is low, it leads to unemployment; to increase employment, the government must boost aggregate demand through investment.[span_69](end_span)

23. Examine the relationship between inflation and unemployment with the help of Philips curve. [span_70](start_span)Also distinguish between short-run and long-run Philips curves.[span_70](end_span)

Philips Curve Relationship:
Developed by A.W. Phillips, it suggests an inverse relationship between the rate of unemployment and the rate of inflation. [span_71](start_span)When unemployment is low, inflation tends to be high, and vice versa.[span_71](end_span)

Short-run vs. Long-run:

    [span_72](start_span)
  • Short-run Philips Curve (SRPC): It is downward sloping, showing the trade-off between inflation and unemployment.[span_72](end_span)
  • Long-run Philips Curve (LRPC): It is a vertical line at the Natural Rate of Unemployment. [span_73](start_span)It suggests that in the long run, there is no trade-off, and inflation depends solely on money supply growth.[span_73](end_span)

24. Define disequilibrium in BOP. Briefly explain the causes of disequilibrium in BOP. [span_74](start_span)Also suggest some remedial measures.[span_74](end_span)

Definition:
[span_75](start_span)BOP disequilibrium occurs when the total receipts from foreigners (credits) do not equal total payments to foreigners (debits) over a period.[span_75](end_span)

Causes:

    [span_76](start_span)
  • Economic Factors: Developmental expenditure, business cycles (recession/boom), and inflation.[span_76](end_span)
    • [span_77](start_span)
  • Political Factors: Political instability or changes in government policies.[span_77](end_span)
    • [span_78](start_span)
  • Social Factors: Changes in tastes, preferences, or population growth.[span_78](end_span)

Remedial Measures:

    [span_79](start_span)
  • Export Promotion: Providing subsidies to exporters.[span_79](end_span)
    • [span_80](start_span)
  • Import Substitution: Encouraging domestic production of imported goods.[span_80](end_span)
    • [span_81](start_span)
  • Exchange Control: Restricting the use of foreign exchange.[span_81](end_span)
    • [span_82](start_span)
  • Devaluation: Reducing the value of domestic currency to make exports cheaper and imports dearer.[span_82](end_span)

Exam Focus Enhancements

Important Formulas List

  • GDP Deflator: (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) x 100
  • Multiplier (K): 1 / (1 - MPC) or 1 / MPS
  • Money Supply (M1): Currency with public + Demand Deposits + Other deposits with RBI

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing **BOT** (only goods) with **BOP** (goods, services, and transfers).
  • In the **Keynesian Multiplier**, forgetting that as MPS (Savings) increases, the multiplier value decreases.
