    6. [span_41](start_span)

(a) Describe the endocrine role of the pancreas. [span_47](start_span)Give an account of the functions of insulin.[span_47](end_span)

[span_48](start_span)

The pancreas acts as an endocrine gland through the Islets of Langerhans.[span_48](end_span) [span_49](start_span)These contain Alpha cells (secreting glucagon) and Beta cells (secreting insulin).[span_49](end_span)

Functions of Insulin:

    [span_50](start_span)
  • Lowers blood glucose levels by promoting glucose uptake by cells.[span_50](end_span)
    • [span_51](start_span)
  • Stimulates glycogenesis (conversion of glucose to glycogen) in the liver and muscles.[span_51](end_span)
    • [span_52](start_span)
  • Inhibits gluconeogenesis and glycogenolysis.[span_52](end_span)
