[span_1](start_span)

Question 2 (b) 5 Marks

Explain Kirchhoff's current law (KCL).

Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL) states that the total current entering a junction or node is exactly equal to the total current leaving that node. [span_5](start_span)This is based on the Law of Conservation of Charge.[span_5](end_span)

Sum of I_in = Sum of I_out

Question 3 (a) 5 Marks

For the given circuit, find the value of total resistance and value of branch currents I1, I2 and I3.

Circuit Data: Voltage (V) = 6V. Series resistor = 0.2 ohm. [span_6](start_span)Parallel resistors: R1 = 2 ohm, R2 = 3 ohm, R3 = 6 ohm.[span_6](end_span)

  1. Parallel Resistance (Rp):
    1/Rp = 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = (3 + 2 + 1)/6 = 6/6 = 1.
    [span_7](start_span)So, Rp = 1 ohm.[span_7](end_span)
  2. Total Resistance (Rt):
    [span_8](start_span)Rt = Series resistor + Rp = 0.2 + 1 = 1.2 ohm.[span_8](end_span)
  3. Total Current (It):
    It = V / Rt = 6 / 1.2 = 5 A.
  4. Branch Currents: Since the voltage across the parallel group is Vp = It * Rp = 5 * 1 = 5V:
    I1 = 5V / 2 ohm = 2.5 A
    I2 = 5V / 3 ohm = 1.67 A
    [span_9](start_span)I3 = 5V / 6 ohm = 0.83 A[span_9](end_span)

A transformer works on the principle of Faraday's Law of Mutual Induction. [span_21](start_span)When an alternating current flows through the primary coil, it creates a changing magnetic flux, which induces an e.m.f. in the secondary coil.[span_21](end_span)

Question 15 (a) 5 Marks

What does the term overloading mean and how can it be prevented?

[span_24](start_span)

Overloading: This occurs when too many electrical appliances are connected to a single socket or circuit, drawing current beyond its rated capacity.[span_24](end_span)

Prevention:

    [span_25](start_span)
  • Avoid using multi-plug adapters for high-power devices.[span_25](end_span)
    • [span_26](start_span)
  • Use Circuit Breakers (MCBs) or fuses of appropriate ratings.[span_26](end_span)
    • [span_27](start_span)
