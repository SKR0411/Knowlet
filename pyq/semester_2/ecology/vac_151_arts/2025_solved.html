What is biogeochemical cycle? Mention types.

[span_6](start_span)

A biogeochemical cycle is the movement of chemical elements and compounds between living organisms and the physical environment.[span_6](end_span) The two main types are:

    [span_7](start_span)
  1. Gaseous Cycles: Such as the Nitrogen and Carbon cycles.[span_7](end_span)
    2. [span_8](start_span)
  2. Sedimentary Cycles: Such as the Phosphorus and Sulphur cycles.[span_8](end_span)

Question 2 (b) [OR] [2 Marks]

What is food web? Give example.

[span_9](start_span)

A food web is a complex network of interconnected food chains in an ecosystem.[span_9](end_span) [span_10](start_span)Example: In a forest, a single plant may be eaten by a grasshopper, a rabbit, or a deer, which are in turn eaten by various predators like owls, foxes, or hawks.[span_10](end_span)

Question 3 (a) [8 Marks]

Define ecosystem. Explain how the flow of energy takes place.

[span_11](start_span)

An ecosystem is a functional unit of nature where living organisms interact with each other and their physical environment.[span_11](end_span) Energy flow follows the 10% Law:

    [span_12](start_span)
  • Energy enters via the Sun and is captured by producers (plants).[span_12](end_span)
    • [span_13](start_span)
  • It moves to primary consumers (herbivores), then secondary and tertiary consumers.[span_13](end_span)
    • [span_14](start_span)
  • Energy flow is unidirectional and decreases at each level as energy is lost as heat.[span_14](end_span)

Question 3 (b) [OR] [8 Marks]

What is ecological succession? Discuss types with examples.

[span_15](start_span)

Ecological succession is the gradual and predictable change in the species composition of a given area.[span_15](end_span) Types include:

    [span_16](start_span)
  • Primary Succession: Starts in a barren area where no life existed (e.g., bare rock, newly formed volcanic island).[span_16](end_span)
    • [span_17](start_span)
  • Secondary Succession: Occurs in areas where a community was destroyed but soil remains (e.g., abandoned farmland, burnt forest).[span_17](end_span)

Question 6 (b) [OR] [8 Marks]

What is deforestation? Describe causes and effects in India.

[span_22](start_span)

Deforestation is the permanent removal of trees to make room for something besides forest.[span_22](end_span)

    [span_23](start_span)
  • Causes: Agricultural expansion, shifting cultivation (Jhum), logging for timber, and infrastructure projects like dams.[span_23](end_span)
    • [span_24](start_span)
  • Effects: Soil erosion, loss of biodiversity, disruption of water cycles, and increased greenhouse gases.[span_24](end_span)

UNIT—III

Question 9 (a) [8 Marks]

Define biodiversity. Mention levels. Discuss biodiversity of N-E India.

[span_25](start_span)

Biodiversity is the variety of life at all levels of biological organization.[span_25](end_span) [span_26](start_span)Levels include Genetic, Species, and Ecosystem diversity.[span_26](end_span) [span_27](start_span)North-East India: This region is a global biodiversity hotspot, home to unique species like the One-horned Rhino and Hoolock Gibbon, and characterized by diverse forest types and high endemism.[span_27](end_span)

Question 9 (b) [OR] [8 Marks]

Define biodiversity hotspots. Discuss global biodiversity hotspots.

[span_28](start_span)

Hotspots are regions with high levels of species richness and endemism that are under significant threat from human activity.[span_28](end_span) [span_29](start_span)Globally, there are around 36 recognized hotspots, including the Western Ghats and the Himalayas in India, which contain a high percentage of the world's plant species as endemics.[span_29](end_span)

UNIT—IV

Question 12 (b) [8 Marks]

What is climate change? Discuss causes and effects.

[span_30](start_span)

Climate change refers to significant, long-term shifts in global weather patterns and temperatures.[span_30](end_span)

    [span_31](start_span)
  • Causes: Greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.[span_31](end_span)
    • [span_32](start_span)
  • Effects: Rising sea levels, melting glaciers, extreme weather events (floods, droughts), and shifts in ecosystems.[span_32](end_span)

What is environmental movement? Write a note on Chipko Movement.

[span_33](start_span)

An environmental movement is a social/political movement for the conservation of the environment.[span_33](end_span) [span_34](start_span)Chipko Movement: Started in the 1970s in Uttarakhand, villagers (led by women like Gaura Devi) hugged trees to prevent them from being felled by contractors.[span_34](end_span) [span_35](start_span)It emphasized the link between forest conservation and the livelihoods of local communities.[span_35](end_span)
