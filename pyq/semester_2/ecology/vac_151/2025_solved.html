Question 1 (Answer any four) [4 x 1 = 4 Marks]

[span_1](start_span)

(a) Who coined the term 'succession'?
The term 'succession' was first used by Adolphe Dureau de la Malle in 1825, though the modern ecological concept was later refined by Frederic Clements[span_1](end_span).

[span_2](start_span)

(b) Define ecosystem.
An ecosystem is a structural and functional unit of the biosphere consisting of a community of living beings and the physical environment, both interacting and exchanging materials between them[span_2](end_span).

(c) Give two examples of lentic ecosystem.
Lentic ecosystems refer to standing water bodies. [span_3](start_span)Examples include Ponds and Lakes[span_3](end_span).

[span_4](start_span)

(d) Name the main source of energy in an ecosystem.
The Sun is the ultimate source of energy for all ecosystems[span_4](end_span).

[span_5](start_span)

(e) Give an example of gaseous type of biogeochemical cycle.
The Nitrogen Cycle or Carbon Cycle[span_5](end_span).

Question 2 [2 Marks]

Option A

What is food web? [span_6](start_span)Give example.
A food web is a network of several interconnected food chains in an ecosystem[span_6](end_span). [span_7](start_span)In a forest, a plant might be eaten by a deer, a rabbit, or a grasshopper, and each of these might be eaten by multiple different predators like wolves or hawks, creating a web-like structure[span_7](end_span).

Option B
[span_8](start_span)

Differentiate between primary and secondary successions.
Primary succession occurs in areas where no life or soil existed before (e.g., bare rock)[span_8](end_span). [span_9](start_span)Secondary succession occurs in areas where a community has been destroyed but the soil remains (e.g., after a forest fire or abandoned farmland)[span_9](end_span).

Question 3 [8 Marks]

Option A

What is ecological pyramid? Describe its types with examples.

[span_10](start_span)

An ecological pyramid is a graphic representation of the relationship between different organisms at various trophic levels[span_10](end_span).

  • Pyramid of Numbers: Represents the number of individuals at each trophic level. [span_11](start_span)Example: A grassland pyramid where grasses are numerous and predators are few[span_11](end_span).
  • Pyramid of Biomass: Represents the total living material (dry weight) at each level. [span_12](start_span)Example: A forest (upright) or a pond (often inverted)[span_12](end_span).
  • Pyramid of Energy: Represents the amount of energy at each level. [span_13](start_span)It is always upright because energy is lost as heat at each transfer[span_13](end_span).
Option B

Write short notes on: (i) Forest ecosystem, (ii) Pond ecosystem.

(i) Forest Ecosystem: A terrestrial ecosystem characterized by high tree density and distinct layers (canopy, understory, floor). [span_14](start_span)It supports high biodiversity and regulates climate[span_14](end_span).
(ii) Pond Ecosystem: A freshwater lentic (standing water) ecosystem. [span_15](start_span)It consists of producers (algae), consumers (fish, insects), and decomposers at the bottom mud[span_15](end_span).

Question 5 [2 Marks]

Option A
[span_21](start_span)

Differentiate between renewable and non-renewable resources with example.
Renewable resources are those that can be replenished naturally over time (e.g., Solar energy, Wind energy)[span_21](end_span). [span_22](start_span)Non-renewable resources exist in finite amounts and cannot be replaced once exhausted (e.g., Coal, Petroleum)[span_22](end_span).

Option B
[span_23](start_span)

What is soil erosion?
Soil erosion is the process by which the top layer of soil is removed by natural physical forces like water and wind or through human activities like deforestation[span_23](end_span).

Question 6 [8 Marks]

Option A

Give a detailed account on the use of surface and groundwater.

[span_24](start_span)

Surface water (rivers, lakes) and groundwater (aquifers) are essential for life[span_24](end_span). [span_25](start_span)Surface water is primarily used for large-scale irrigation, industrial cooling, and hydroelectric power[span_25](end_span). [span_26](start_span)Groundwater is a major source of drinking water in rural areas and is heavily used for agriculture via tubewells, though over-extraction is leading to falling water tables[span_26](end_span).

Option B

Give a note on alternative sources of energy resources.

[span_27](start_span)

Alternative or non-conventional energy sources are sustainable and eco-friendly[span_27](end_span). Key sources include:
1. Solar Energy: Using PV cells to convert sunlight into electricity.
2. Wind Energy: Using turbines in high-wind areas.
3. Geothermal Energy: Tapping into heat from the Earth's interior.
4. [span_28](start_span)Biomass: Energy from organic waste[span_28](end_span).
