Question 2 (a) [2 Marks]

Write a note on shifting cultivation:

[span_8](start_span)

Shifting cultivation, also known as "Slash and Burn" or "Jhum" in Northeast India, involves clearing a patch of forest land by cutting and burning trees[span_8](end_span). [span_9](start_span)Crops are grown for a few years until the soil fertility declines, after which the cultivators move to a new patch, allowing the old one to lie fallow and regenerate[span_9](end_span).

Question 2 (b) [Option B] [2 Marks]

Functions of Joint Forest Management (JFM):

[span_10](start_span)

JFM aims to protect and manage forests through a partnership between local communities and the forest department[span_10](end_span). [span_11](start_span)Its primary functions include protecting forest lands from illegal encroachment, promoting sustainable harvesting of non-timber forest products, and facilitating community-based afforestation programs[span_11](end_span).

[span_32](start_span)

[span_35](start_span)

Definition: Presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere that affect human health and the environment[span_35](end_span).

[span_36](start_span)

Causes: Burning of fossil fuels, industrial emissions, forest fires, and vehicular exhaust[span_36](end_span).

[span_37](start_span)

[span_38](start_span)

[span_39](start_span)

[span_40](start_span)

Effects: Increased skin cancer, cataracts, damage to immune systems, and harmful impacts on marine phytoplankton[span_40](end_span).

Question 15 (a) [8 Marks]

Impact of Industry on Environment:

[span_43](start_span)

Industries contribute to environmental degradation through air and water pollution, heavy metal contamination, and excessive resource consumption[span_43](end_span). [span_44](start_span)Industrial runoff often destroys aquatic life, while smoke emissions contribute to acid rain and global warming[span_44](end_span).

Question 15 (b) [Option B] [8 Marks]

Impact of Modern Agriculture on Environment:

[span_45](start_span)

Modern agriculture uses high-yield variety seeds, chemical fertilizers, and pesticides[span_45](end_span). [span_46](start_span)While increasing food production, it leads to soil degradation, water pollution (eutrophication), loss of traditional crop varieties, and groundwater depletion[span_46](end_span).
