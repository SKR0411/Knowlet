SECTION-A: Very Short Answer Questions

Answer any twenty questions. (1 x 20 = 20 Marks)

1. Define resource. 1 Mark

[span_0](start_span)

A resource is any physical material from the Earth that people need and value, such as water, air, iron ore, or even information and human labor[span_0](end_span).

2. What are the types of forest resources? 1 Mark

[span_1](start_span)[span_2](start_span)

Forest resources are primarily categorized into Timber (wood for construction/fuel) and Non-Timber Forest Products (NTFP) such as fruits, gums, resins, and medicinal plants[span_1](end_span)[span_2](end_span).

3. Define shifting cultivation. 1 Mark

[span_3](start_span)
Shifting cultivation is an agricultural system in which plots of land are cultivated temporarily, then abandoned while post-settlement vegetation is allowed to grow freely while the cultivator moves to another plot[span_3](end_span).

4. What do you mean by fossil fuels? 1 Mark

[span_4](start_span)

Fossil fuels are natural fuels such as coal or gas, formed in the geological past from the remains of living organisms[span_4](end_span).

5. Write the full form of IFM. 1 Mark

[span_5](start_span)

The full form of IFM is Integrated Forest Management[span_5](end_span).

6. Define conservation. 1 Mark

[span_6](start_span)

Conservation is the sustainable use and management of natural resources including wildlife, water, air, and earth deposits to prevent exploitation or destruction[span_6](end_span).

7. What are biodiversity hotspots? 1 Mark

[span_7](start_span)

Biodiversity hotspots are biogeographic regions that are both a significant reservoir of biodiversity and are threatened with destruction[span_7](end_span).

8. What is ex-situ conservation? 1 Mark

[span_8](start_span)[span_9](start_span)

Ex-situ conservation is the technique of conservation of components of biological diversity outside their natural habitats (e.g., zoos, botanical gardens)[span_8](end_span)[span_9](end_span).

9. Write down the full form of IUCN. 1 Mark

[span_10](start_span)

The full form of IUCN is International Union for Conservation of Nature[span_10](end_span).

10. In which year was Red Data Book published? 1 Mark

[span_11](start_span)

The first Red Data Book was published in 1964[span_11](end_span).

11. Define pollution. 1 Mark

[span_12](start_span)

Pollution is the introduction of harmful materials (pollutants) into the environment that cause adverse change[span_12](end_span).

12. What are pollutants? 1 Mark

[span_13](start_span)

Pollutants are substances or energies introduced into the environment that have undesired effects or adversely affect the usefulness of a resource[span_13](end_span).

13. What are the main causes of air pollution? 1 Mark

[span_14](start_span)

Main causes include combustion of fossil fuels, industrial emissions, wildfires, and vehicular exhaust[span_14](end_span).

14. Define photochemical smog. 1 Mark

[span_15](start_span)

Photochemical smog is a type of air pollution derived from vehicle emissions from internal combustion engines and industrial fumes that react in the atmosphere with sunlight to form secondary pollutants[span_15](end_span).

15. Write down the full form of SPM. 1 Mark

[span_16](start_span)

The full form of SPM is Suspended Particulate Matter[span_16](end_span).

16. Define climate mitigation. 1 Mark

[span_17](start_span)

Climate mitigation consists of actions to limit the magnitude or rate of long-term climate change, primarily by reducing greenhouse gas emissions[span_17](end_span).

17. Write down the names of greenhouse gases. 1 Mark

[span_18](start_span)[span_19](start_span)

Common greenhouse gases include Carbon dioxide (CO2), Methane (CH4), Nitrous oxide (N2O), and Water vapor[span_18](end_span)[span_19](end_span).

18. Define acid rain. 1 Mark

[span_20](start_span)

Acid rain is rainfall made sufficiently acidic by atmospheric pollution (primarily from sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides) that it causes environmental harm[span_20](end_span).

19. Write down the function of ozone layer. 1 Mark

[span_21](start_span)

The ozone layer acts as a shield for life on Earth by absorbing most of the Sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation[span_21](end_span).

20. What are the causes of Bhopal Gas Tragedy? 1 Mark

[span_22](start_span)[span_23](start_span)

The primary cause was the leakage of Methyl Isocyanate (MIC) gas from a Union Carbide pesticide plant due to water entering a storage tank[span_22](end_span)[span_23](end_span).

21. Define urban sprawl. 1 Mark

[span_24](start_span)[span_25](start_span)

Urban sprawl refers to the rapid, often unplanned expansion of cities and towns into surrounding rural areas[span_24](end_span)[span_25](end_span).

22. What is traffic congestion? 1 Mark

[span_26](start_span)

Traffic congestion is a condition on transport networks that occurs as use increases, characterized by slower speeds and increased vehicular queueing[span_26](end_span).

23. Define environmental degradation. 1 Mark

[span_27](start_span)

Environmental degradation is the deterioration of the environment through depletion of resources such as air, water, and soil, and the destruction of ecosystems[span_27](end_span).

24. How does technology affect the environment? 1 Mark

[span_28](start_span)

Technology can affect the environment both negatively (e.g., pollution, resource depletion) and positively (e.g., renewable energy, conservation monitoring)[span_28](end_span).

25. What is resettlement? 1 Mark

[span_29](start_span)

Resettlement is the act or instance of settling again, especially the relocation of people who have been displaced by projects like dams or urban development[span_29](end_span).
