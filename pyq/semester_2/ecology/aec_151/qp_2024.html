36. What is ecological pyramid? Discuss the different types of ecological pyramids with suitable examples. [1+7=8]

[span_40](start_span)

An ecological pyramid is a graphical representation of the relationship between different organisms in an ecosystem at various trophic levels[span_40](end_span).

    [span_41](start_span)
  1. Pyramid of Number: Shows the number of individual organisms at each trophic level (e.g., Upright in grasslands)[span_41](end_span).
    2. [span_42](start_span)
  2. Pyramid of Biomass: Shows the total dry weight of living matter at each level (e.g., Upright in terrestrial, but can be inverted in aquatic ecosystems)[span_42](end_span).
  3. Pyramid of Energy: Shows the rate of energy flow. [span_43](start_span)This is always upright because energy is lost as heat at each transfer[span_43](end_span).

37. Define biogeochemical cycle. Illustrate the nitrogen cycle with a diagram. [2+6=8]

[span_44](start_span)

A biogeochemical cycle is the movement of chemical elements and compounds between living organisms and the physical environment (atmosphere, soil, water)[span_44](end_span).

[span_45](start_span)

Steps of Nitrogen Cycle: Nitrogen fixation (atmospheric N2 to ammonia), Nitrification (ammonia to nitrites/nitrates), Assimilation (plants taking up nitrogen), Ammonification (conversion of waste to ammonia), and Denitrification (nitrates back to N2 gas)[span_45](end_span).
