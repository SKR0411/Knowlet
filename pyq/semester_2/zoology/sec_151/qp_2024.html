31. What is ESR? Write a note on the principle of ESR and its significance. 1+4=5

[span_47](start_span)

ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate) is the rate at which red blood cells in anticoagulated whole blood descend in a standardized tube over a period of one hour[span_47](end_span).

[span_48](start_span)

Principle: When an inflammatory process is present, high levels of fibrinogen cause RBCs to stick together and form stacks called rouleaux, which settle faster due to increased density[span_48](end_span).

[span_49](start_span)

Significance: It is a non-specific marker of inflammation and is used to monitor conditions like rheumatoid arthritis or systemic lupus erythematosus[span_49](end_span).
