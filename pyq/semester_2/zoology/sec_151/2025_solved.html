Question 3 (Answer any one) 5 Marks

Option A: Define PCV. [span_17](start_span)Write a note on principles, procedure and significance of platelet count using haemocytometer.[span_17](end_span)

[span_18](start_span)

Definition of PCV: Packed Cell Volume (PCV), also known as hematocrit, is the measurement of the proportion of blood that is made up of red blood cells.[span_18](end_span)

Platelet Count using Haemocytometer:

  • Principle: Whole blood is diluted with a specific diluting fluid (like Rees-Ecker solution) that preserves platelets while lysing other cells. [span_19](start_span)The platelets are then counted in a specific volume using a Neubauer counting chamber.[span_19](end_span)
  • Procedure: Blood is drawn to the 0.5 mark in a Thoma pipette and diluted to the 101 mark. After mixing, a small amount is charged into the haemocytometer. [span_20](start_span)Platelets are allowed to settle and then counted under a microscope in the central large square.[span_20](end_span)
    • [span_21](start_span)
  • Significance: Essential for diagnosing bleeding disorders, thrombocytopenia (low count), or thrombocytosis (high count).[span_21](end_span)

Option B: Mention the names of two stains used for staining blood smear. [span_22](start_span)Add a brief note on procedure and significance of DLC.[span_22](end_span)

[span_23](start_span)

Stains: 1. Leishman's Stain, 2. Giemsa Stain.[span_23](end_span)

Differential Leucocyte Count (DLC):

  • Procedure: A thin blood film is prepared on a glass slide, dried, and fixed with methanol or stained directly. [span_24](start_span)The slide is examined under oil immersion, and 100 WBCs are identified and categorized (Neutrophils, Lymphocytes, Monocytes, Eosinophils, Basophils).[span_24](end_span)
    • [span_25](start_span)
  • Significance: Helps detect specific types of infections, leukemias, and allergic reactions based on which specific WBC type is elevated.[span_25](end_span)
