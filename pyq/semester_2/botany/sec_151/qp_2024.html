SECTION-A (Answer any fifteen) 1 × 15 = 15

1. Define mushrooms.

[span_3](start_span)

Mushrooms are the macroscopic fruiting bodies of certain fungi, typically belonging to the Basidiomycota or Ascomycota divisions, which can be either edible or poisonous[span_3](end_span).

2. What do you mean by 'toadstools'?

[span_4](start_span)

The term 'toadstools' is commonly used to refer to poisonous or inedible mushrooms, often characterized by bright colors or umbrella-like caps[span_4](end_span).

3. What is button mushroom?

[span_5](start_span)

Button mushroom refers to Agaricus bisporus, the most widely cultivated and consumed mushroom globally, known for its white, rounded cap[span_5](end_span).

4. What is oyster mushroom?

[span_6](start_span)

Oyster mushroom refers to species of Pleurotus, named for their oyster-shaped caps and rapid growth on various lignocellulosic substrates[span_6](end_span).

5. What is inoculation hook?

[span_7](start_span)

An inoculation hook is a laboratory tool with a bent metal tip used to transfer small pieces of fungal mycelium or agar into growth media[span_7](end_span).

6. Define inoculation loop.

[span_8](start_span)

An inoculation loop consists of a handle with a thin wire forming a small loop at the end, used to pick up and transfer microbial cultures or spores[span_8](end_span).

7. Define culture rack.

[span_9](start_span)

A culture rack is a tiered structure used in laboratory or cultivation rooms to organize and hold Petri dishes, flasks, or spawn bottles during incubation[span_9](end_span).

8. Write about low-cost stove.

[span_10](start_span)

A low-cost stove is a simple heating device used in small-scale mushroom units for sterilizing substrates or boiling grains for spawn preparation[span_10](end_span).

9. What is mushroom bed?

[span_11](start_span)

A mushroom bed is a prepared surface or container filled with sterilized or pasteurized substrate where fungal mycelium is grown for fruiting[span_11](end_span).

10. Define composting.

[span_12](start_span)

Composting is the biological process of decomposing organic matter (like straw and manure) by microbes to create a nutrient-rich substrate suitable for button mushrooms[span_12](end_span).

11. Write about maize straw for bed preparation.

[span_13](start_span)

Maize straw serves as a locally available lignocellulosic substrate that can be chopped and soaked to prepare beds for oyster mushroom cultivation[span_13](end_span).

12. What do you mean by low-cost technology?

[span_14](start_span)

Low-cost technology involves using inexpensive, locally available materials and manual methods to cultivate mushrooms without high investment in heavy machinery[span_14](end_span).

13. Define storage of mushrooms.

[span_15](start_span)

Storage refers to the techniques used to preserve the shelf life of harvested mushrooms, preventing spoilage due to their high moisture content[span_15](end_span).

14. What is canning?

[span_16](start_span)

Canning is a long-term preservation method where mushrooms are cleaned, blanched, and sealed in airtight cans with brine, followed by heat sterilization[span_16](end_span).

15. Name two amino acids present in mushrooms.

[span_17](start_span)

Lysine and Tryptophan[span_17](end_span).

16. Name some mineral elements present in mushrooms.

[span_18](start_span)

Potassium, Phosphorus, Magnesium, and Selenium[span_18](end_span).

17. Name a mushroom toxin.

[span_19](start_span)

α-Amanitin (found in Amanita phalloides)[span_19](end_span).

18. Name one mushroom research centre at National level.

[span_20](start_span)
ICAR-Directorate of Mushroom Research (DMR), Solan, Himachal Pradesh[span_20](end_span).

19. Name one mushroom research centre at regional level.

[span_21](start_span)

Mushroom Development Foundation (MDF), Guwahati (Regional example for NE India)[span_21](end_span).

20. Why is mushroom regarded as an ideal food for diabetic patients?

[span_22](start_span)

Mushrooms are ideal because they are low in calories, low in sugar/starch, and contain no cholesterol, helping maintain stable blood glucose levels[span_22](end_span).

25. Give a brief account of bed preparation with paddy straw. [2]

[span_30](start_span)

Paddy straw is soaked in water (sometimes with lime) for 12-24 hours, drained to maintain 65% moisture, and then layered in blocks or bags while mixing in mushroom spawn[span_30](end_span).

[span_31](start_span)

Drying involves removing moisture either by sun-drying or using mechanical dehydrators[span_31](end_span). [span_32](start_span)It reduces the water activity, allowing mushrooms to be stored for several months without spoilage[span_32](end_span).

32. Write an essay on Agaricus bisporus. [5]

[span_35](start_span)

Introduction: Agaricus bisporus, commonly known as the White Button Mushroom, is the most commercially cultivated species worldwide[span_35](end_span).

Cultivation Steps:

    [span_36](start_span)
  1. Composting: Preparing a specific substrate using straw, horse manure, and gypsum[span_36](end_span).
    2. [span_37](start_span)
  2. Spawning: Inoculating the compost with pure mushroom culture (spawn)[span_37](end_span).
    3. [span_38](start_span)
  3. Casing: Covering the colonized compost with a layer of soil/peat to induce fruiting[span_38](end_span).
    4. [span_39](start_span)
  4. Cropping: Harvesting the mushrooms at the "button" stage[span_39](end_span).

34. Describe the preparation of spawn in detail. [5]

[span_40](start_span)

Spawn is the "seed" of the mushroom[span_40](end_span). Its preparation involves:

    [span_41](start_span)
  • Grain Selection: Using wheat, sorghum, or pearl millet grains[span_41](end_span).
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • Boiling and Sterilization: Grains are boiled, mixed with Calcium Carbonate, and autoclaved at 121°C[span_42](end_span).
    • [span_43](start_span)
  • Inoculation: Pure culture of the desired mushroom is added to the grains under sterile conditions[span_43](end_span).
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • Incubation: Kept in a dark room until the mycelium completely covers the grains[span_44](end_span).

36. Describe mushroom composting. [5]

Composting creates a selective substrate for Agaricus. It is done in two phases:

  • Phase I (Outdoor): Raw materials like straw and poultry manure are wetted and piled. [span_45](start_span)Microbes begin fermentation, raising temperatures to 70°C[span_45](end_span).
    • [span_46](start_span)
  • Phase II (Indoor/Pasteurization): The compost is heat-treated to kill pests and pathogens while converting ammonia into microbial protein[span_46](end_span).

38. Describe crude fibre and vitamins in mushrooms. [3+2=5]

[span_47](start_span)

Crude Fibre: Mushrooms contain significant amounts of chitin and hemicellulose in their cell walls, acting as dietary fiber that aids digestion and prevents constipation[span_47](end_span).

[span_48](start_span)

Vitamins: They are excellent sources of Vitamin B-complex (Riboflavin, Niacin) and Vitamin D[span_48](end_span). [span_49](start_span)Some species also provide Vitamin C and Folate[span_49](end_span).
