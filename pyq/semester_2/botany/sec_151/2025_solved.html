Question 6(a) [5 Marks]

[span_30](start_span)

Give a detailed account of pure culture.[span_30](end_span)

[span_31](start_span)Pure Culture: A population of cells or multicellular organisms growing in the absence of other species or types.[span_31](end_span)

In mushroom cultivation, pure culture is essential for producing high-quality spawn:

    [span_32](start_span)[span_33](start_span)
  • Isolation: Obtained using tissue culture from a healthy mushroom fruiting body or spore culture.[span_32](end_span)[span_33](end_span)
    • [span_34](start_span)
  • Media: Typically grown on Potato Dextrose Agar (PDA) or Malt Extract Agar (MEA).[span_34](end_span)
    • [span_35](start_span)
  • Maintenance: Requires strict aseptic conditions to prevent contamination by molds or bacteria.[span_35](end_span)
    • [span_36](start_span)
  • Purpose: Ensures the genetic uniformity and vigor of the cultivated mushroom.[span_36](end_span)

Long-term storage is vital to handle the highly perishable nature of mushrooms:

    [span_53](start_span)
  • Drying/Dehydration: Sun-drying or mechanical drying to reduce moisture below 10%.[span_53](end_span)
    • [span_54](start_span)[span_55](start_span)
  • Canning: Storing in brine solution within tin cans for 1-2 years of shelf life.[span_54](end_span)[span_55](end_span)
    • [span_56](start_span)
  • Freezing: Blanching followed by quick-freezing at -18°C.[span_56](end_span)
    • [span_57](start_span)
  • Pickling: Preserving in vinegar or oil with spices.[span_57](end_span)
