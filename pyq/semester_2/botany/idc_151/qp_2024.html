26. Short note on acid rain.

[span_25](start_span)

Acid rain is caused by chemical reactions when compounds like sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides are released into the air[span_25](end_span). [span_26](start_span)These substances can rise very high into the atmosphere, where they mix and react with water, oxygen, and other chemicals to form acidic pollutants[span_26](end_span). [span_27](start_span)It damages forests, aquatic life, and buildings[span_27](end_span).

27. Short note on ozone layer depletion.

[span_28](start_span)

Ozone layer depletion is the thinning of the ozone layer in the upper atmosphere[span_28](end_span). [span_29](start_span)It is primarily caused by man-made chemicals like chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)[span_29](end_span). [span_30](start_span)This depletion allows more Ultraviolet (UV) radiation to reach the Earth, increasing risks of skin cancer and eye damage[span_30](end_span).

37. Describe the levels of biodiversity. Note on alpha, beta and gamma diversity. [4+4]

[span_38](start_span)

Levels of Biodiversity: Biodiversity is typically observed at three levels[span_38](end_span):

    [span_39](start_span)
  • Genetic Diversity: Variation in genes within a single species[span_39](end_span).
    • [span_40](start_span)
  • Species Diversity: The variety of species within a habitat or region[span_40](end_span).
    • [span_41](start_span)
  • Ecosystem Diversity: The variety of ecosystems in a broad geographic area[span_41](end_span).

Whittaker's Diversity Measures:

    [span_42](start_span)
  • Alpha Diversity: Diversity within a particular area or ecosystem (local diversity)[span_42](end_span).
    • [span_43](start_span)
  • Beta Diversity: The variation of species composition between different ecosystems[span_43](end_span).
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • Gamma Diversity: The overall diversity for the different ecosystems within a large region[span_44](end_span).

40. Why is India a megabiodiversity nation? Give account of biodiversity of India. [4+4]

[span_45](start_span)

India is recognized as one of the 17 megabiodiversity countries in the world because it harbors a high percentage of the world's species[span_45](end_span).

    [span_46](start_span)
  • Geographic Diversity: India has diverse landscapes ranging from the Himalayas to the Thar Desert and coastal regions[span_46](end_span).
    • [span_47](start_span)
  • Species Richness: It accounts for nearly 7-8% of all recorded species globally[span_47](end_span).
    • [span_48](start_span)
  • Endemism: A large number of species, especially in the Western Ghats and Himalayas, are found nowhere else[span_48](end_span).
    • [span_49](start_span)
  • Cultural Link: Traditional knowledge and sacred groves contribute to preserving biodiversity[span_49](end_span).

41. Characteristics of biodiversity hotspots of India with examples. [8]

[span_50](start_span)

Biodiversity hotspots are regions that contain high levels of species richness and a high degree of endemism, but are also under significant threat[span_50](end_span).

Characteristics:

    [span_51](start_span)
  • Must contain at least 1,500 species of vascular plants as endemics[span_51](end_span).
    • [span_52](start_span)
  • Must have lost at least 70% of its original native vegetation[span_52](end_span).

Examples in India:

    [span_53](start_span)
  • Himalayas: Home to species like the Snow Leopard and numerous medicinal plants[span_53](end_span).
    • [span_54](start_span)
  • Western Ghats: Known for high amphibian and plant diversity; includes Silent Valley[span_54](end_span).
    • [span_55](start_span)
  • Indo-Burma: Covers parts of Northeast India[span_55](end_span).
    • [span_56](start_span)
  • Sundaland: Includes the Nicobar Islands[span_56](end_span).
