Question 2(a) [10 Marks]

[span_14](start_span)

Describe the different types of modified stems with suitable examples and necessary diagrams.[span_14](end_span)

[span_15](start_span)

Stems are modified to perform diverse functions like storage, support, and protection.[span_15](end_span)

1. Underground Modifications (Storage)

    [span_16](start_span)
  • Tuber: Swollen tip of underground branch (e.g., Potato).[span_16](end_span)
    • [span_17](start_span)
  • Rhizome: Prostrate, thick stem growing horizontally (e.g., Ginger).[span_17](end_span)
    • [span_18](start_span)
  • Bulb: Highly reduced stem surrounded by fleshy leaves (e.g., Onion).[span_18](end_span)

2. Sub-aerial Modifications (Vegetative Propagation)

    [span_19](start_span)
  • Runner: Creeps on the ground (e.g., Grass).[span_19](end_span)
    • [span_20](start_span)
  • Offset: Found in aquatic plants (e.g., Pistia).[span_20](end_span)

3. Aerial Modifications

    [span_21](start_span)
  • Thorns: Hard, woody, pointed structures for protection (e.g., Citrus).[span_21](end_span)
    • [span_22](start_span)
  • Cladode: A phylloclade with limited growth (e.g., Asparagus).[span_22](end_span)

Question 2(b) [10 Marks]

[span_23](start_span)

Describe the structure and types of fruits and seeds with necessary diagrams and suitable examples.[span_23](end_span)

[span_24](start_span)

Fruits: A matured or ripened ovary developed after fertilization.[span_24](end_span)

    [span_25](start_span)
  • Simple Fruits: Develop from a single carpel (e.g., Mango/Drupe).[span_25](end_span)
    • [span_26](start_span)
  • Aggregate Fruits: Develop from polycarpellary apocarpous ovary (e.g., Custard apple).[span_26](end_span)
    • [span_27](start_span)
  • Composite Fruits: Develop from the entire inflorescence (e.g., Pineapple).[span_27](end_span)
[span_28](start_span)

Seeds: The fertilized, matured ovule.[span_28](end_span)

    [span_29](start_span)
  • Dicotyledonous: Two cotyledons (e.g., Pea, Gram).[span_29](end_span)
    • [span_30](start_span)
  • Monocotyledonous: Single cotyledon (e.g., Maize, Rice).[span_30](end_span)
[span_31](start_span)

Structure includes the seed coat (testa and tegmen) and the embryo (radicle and plumule).[span_31](end_span)
