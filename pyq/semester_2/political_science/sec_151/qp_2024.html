33. Write a note on the importance of sampling in social research. [span_87](start_span)[5 Marks][span_87](end_span)

[span_88](start_span)[span_89](start_span)

Sampling is the backbone of modern social research because studying an entire population (census) is often impossible.[span_88](end_span)[span_89](end_span)

    [span_90](start_span)
  • Saves Time and Money: Analyzing a small representative group is significantly cheaper and faster than a full census.[span_90](end_span)
    • [span_91](start_span)
  • Greater Accuracy: With a smaller group, researchers can maintain higher control over the quality of data collection.[span_91](end_span)
    • [span_92](start_span)
  • Feasibility: In cases where a population is infinite or hard to reach, sampling is the only practical option.[span_92](end_span)
    • [span_93](start_span)
  • Detailed Study: Smaller samples allow for more in-depth, intensive investigation of each unit.[span_93](end_span)
