6. [span_50](start_span)Descriptive Questions[span_50](end_span)

[span_51](start_span)

(a) Discuss the types of non-probability sampling.[span_51](end_span)

    [span_52](start_span)
  • Convenience Sampling: Units are selected based on their easy accessibility to the researcher.[span_52](end_span)
    • [span_53](start_span)
  • Quota Sampling: The researcher ensures that certain subgroups are represented in the sample in specific proportions.[span_53](end_span)
    • [span_54](start_span)
  • Judgmental/Purposive Sampling: The researcher uses their own expertise to select units that are most useful for the study.[span_54](end_span)
    • [span_55](start_span)
  • Snowball Sampling: Existing subjects recruit future subjects from among their acquaintances.[span_55](end_span)
[span_56](start_span)

(b) Write notes on the advantages and limitations of sampling.[span_56](end_span)

[span_57](start_span)

Advantages: Economical in terms of money, labor, and time; allows for intensive data collection[span_57](end_span).
[span_58](start_span)Limitations: High chance of bias if the sample is not representative; requires specialized knowledge to design; presence of sampling error.[span_58](end_span)

Exam Focus Enhancements

Exam Tips:

  • For Sampling questions, try to illustrate the concept with a small population/sample diagram.
  • Always mention the specific difference between Questionnaire and Schedule as it is a frequent exam topic.

Common Mistakes:

  • Do not confuse Exit Polls (post-voting) with Opinion Polls (pre-voting).
  • Ensure you distinguish between Coding (assigning symbols) and Tabulation (arranging in tables).
