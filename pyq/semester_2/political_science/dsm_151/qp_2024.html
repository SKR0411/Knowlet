1. What is political theory? [2 Marks]

[span_7](start_span)

Political theory is the study of the concepts and principles that people use to describe, explain, and evaluate political events and institutions[span_7](end_span). It involves a systematic reflection on the nature and purposes of government and the role of individuals within a political community.

2. Write two differences between politics and political science. [2 Marks]

    [span_8](start_span)
  • Nature: Politics is the actual practice of power and decision-making in society, whereas Political Science is the formal, academic study of those practices[span_8](end_span).
    • [span_9](start_span)
  • Focus: Politics often involves personal opinions, conflicts, and immediate actions, while Political Science seeks to be objective, theoretical, and analytical[span_9](end_span).

3. Mention any two importance of political theory. [2 Marks]

    [span_10](start_span)
  • Clarity of Concepts: It helps in defining and understanding fundamental ideas like liberty, equality, and justice[span_10](end_span).
    • [span_11](start_span)
  • Critical Evaluation: It provides a framework to critique current political systems and suggest improvements for a more just society[span_11](end_span).

4. Write any two conditions for the success of democracy. [2 Marks]

    [span_12](start_span)
  • Universal Education: An educated citizenry is essential to understand political issues and make informed choices[span_12](end_span).
    • [span_13](start_span)
  • Freedom of Press: A free and independent media acts as a watchdog, ensuring government transparency and accountability[span_13](end_span).

5. Write a note on gender equality. [2 Marks]

[span_14](start_span)

Gender equality is the state in which access to rights or opportunities is unaffected by gender[span_14](end_span). It advocates for equal social, political, and economic status for all genders, aiming to eliminate discrimination and patriarchal barriers.

6. Write any two features of justice. [2 Marks]

    [span_15](start_span)
  • Impartiality: Justice requires that decisions be made based on objective criteria rather than bias or favoritism[span_15](end_span).
    • [span_16](start_span)
  • Proportionality: It often involves treating people in a way that is proportional to their actions or needs (e.g., equal work for equal pay)[span_16](end_span).

7. What is meant by natural rights? [2 Marks]

[span_17](start_span)

Natural rights are those rights that are believed to belong to all people from birth, regardless of the laws or customs of any particular culture or government[span_17](end_span). They are often considered universal and inalienable, such as the rights to life and liberty.

8. Mention any two distinctions between State and Government. [2 Marks]

    [span_18](start_span)
  • Permanence: The State is a permanent institution, while the Government is temporary and changes periodically through elections[span_18](end_span).
    • [span_19](start_span)
  • Composition: The State consists of the population, territory, government, and sovereignty; the Government is only one of the four essential elements of the State[span_19](end_span).

9. Write any two functions of civil society. [2 Marks]

    [span_20](start_span)

    • [span_21](start_span)
  • Participation: It encourages democratic participation by organizing people to voice their interests and influence public policy[span_21](end_span).

10. What is political debate? [2 Marks]

[span_22](start_span)

Political debate is a formal discussion or argument on a particular matter of public policy or political concern, where different sides present their views to persuade an audience or reach a decision[span_22](end_span).

11. What is Lee thesis? [2 Marks]

[span_23](start_span)

The "Lee Thesis," named after Lee Kuan Yew of Singapore, suggests that authoritarianism and the restriction of certain political rights are sometimes necessary to promote rapid economic growth and stability in developing nations[span_23](end_span).

12. Mention any two types of censorship. [2 Marks]

    [span_24](start_span)

    • [span_25](start_span)
  • Moral Censorship: The removal of materials that are considered obscene or harmful to the moral standards of a society[span_25](end_span).

16. What is Politics? Discuss the characteristics of politics. [2+8=10 Marks]

[span_33](start_span)

Definition: Politics is the process of making collective decisions within a society, typically involving the distribution of resources, power, and status[span_33](end_span).

Characteristics:

    [span_34](start_span)
  • Conflict and Cooperation: Politics exists because people disagree, yet it is the mechanism used to resolve those disagreements[span_34](end_span).
    • [span_35](start_span)
  • Power Centric: At its core, politics is about who has the power to make rules and who must follow them[span_35](end_span).
    • [span_36](start_span)
  • Public Activity: It generally deals with the "res publica" or public affairs, rather than purely private matters[span_36](end_span).
    • [span_37](start_span)
  • Authoritative Allocation: As David Easton defined it, politics is the "authoritative allocation of values" for a society[span_37](end_span).

17. Discuss the nature and scope of political theory. [10 Marks]

Nature: Political theory is both normative (what ought to be) and empirical (what is). [span_38](start_span)It is a tradition of inquiry that dates back to Ancient Greece, focusing on the fundamental questions of political life[span_38](end_span).

Scope:

    [span_39](start_span)
  • Study of Concepts: Analyzing ideas like sovereignty, power, rights, and citizenship[span_39](end_span).
    • [span_40](start_span)
  • Study of Institutions: Examining the origin and nature of the State, Government, and laws[span_40](end_span).
    • [span_41](start_span)
  • Ideological Analysis: Understanding frameworks like Liberalism, Marxism, and Feminism[span_41](end_span).
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • Policy Evaluation: Providing the ethical basis for assessing government actions and societal structures[span_42](end_span).

18. Define democracy. Discuss the merits and demerits of democracy. [2+8=10 Marks]

[span_43](start_span)

Definition: Democracy is a system of government by the whole population or all the eligible members of a state, typically through elected representatives[span_43](end_span).

Merits:

    [span_44](start_span)

  • Accountability: Regular elections ensure that the government remains responsible to the people[span_45](end_span).
    • [span_46](start_span)

Demerits:

    [span_47](start_span)

19. What is liberty? How can liberty be safeguarded? [2+8=10 Marks]

[span_50](start_span)

Definition: Liberty is the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions imposed by authority[span_50](end_span).

Safeguards of Liberty:

    [span_51](start_span)
  • Democratic Form of Government: Democracy is considered a primary safeguard as power rests with the people[span_51](end_span).
    • [span_52](start_span)
  • Constitutional Guarantees: Enshrining fundamental rights in a written constitution protects them from arbitrary changes[span_52](end_span).
    • [span_53](start_span)

20. Discuss the methods of acquisition and loss of citizenship. [10 Marks]

[span_55](start_span)

Citizenship is the legal status of being a member of a state, entailing both rights and duties[span_55](end_span).

Methods of Acquisition:

    [span_56](start_span)

Methods of Loss:

    [span_59](start_span)
  • Renunciation: Voluntarily giving up one's citizenship[span_59](end_span).
    • [span_60](start_span)
  • Termination: Automatically losing citizenship upon voluntarily acquiring the citizenship of another country[span_60](end_span).
    • [span_61](start_span)

21. Write a note on the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. [10 Marks]

[span_62](start_span)

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) was adopted by the United Nations General Assembly in 1948[span_62](end_span).

    [span_63](start_span)
  • Foundation: It was the first global expression of what many believe are the rights to which all human beings are inherently entitled[span_63](end_span).
    • [span_64](start_span)
  • Contents: It consists of 30 articles detailing individual rights, including civil, political, economic, social, and cultural rights[span_64](end_span).
    • [span_65](start_span)
  • Impact: While not a legally binding treaty itself, it has served as the basis for two binding UN human rights covenants and influenced the constitutions of many nations[span_65](end_span).
    • [span_66](start_span)
  • Significance: It establishes a common standard of achievement for all peoples and all nations to promote respect for these rights[span_66](end_span).

[span_67](start_span)

Compatibility Discussion:

    [span_68](start_span)

    • [span_69](start_span)
