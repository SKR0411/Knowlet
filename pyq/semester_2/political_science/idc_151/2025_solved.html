(a) Explain the role of the Constituent Assembly in framing of the Indian Constitution.[span_17](end_span)

[span_18](start_span)

The Constituent Assembly, formed in 1946 under the Cabinet Mission Plan, played a monumental role in drafting the Constitution[span_18](end_span):

    [span_19](start_span)
  • Representation: It consisted of indirectly elected members from provincial assemblies, representing various shades of Indian political thought[span_19](end_span).
  • Drafting Committee: Led by Dr. B.R. [span_20](start_span)[span_21](start_span)Ambedkar, this committee synthesized various global constitutional ideas into the Indian context[span_20](end_span)[span_21](end_span).
    • [span_22](start_span)
  • Deliberation: Every clause was debated extensively over nearly three years (2 years, 11 months, and 18 days) to ensure consensus[span_22](end_span).
[span_23](start_span)

(b) Explain the ideals enshrined in the Preamble of the Indian Constitution.[span_23](end_span)

[span_24](start_span)

The Preamble enshrines several core ideals[span_24](end_span):

    [span_25](start_span)
  • Sovereign: India is independent of any external power[span_25](end_span).
    • [span_26](start_span)
  • Socialist & Secular: Added by the 42nd Amendment, these emphasize economic equality and equal respect for all religions[span_26](end_span).
    • [span_27](start_span)
  • Democratic Republic: Power lies with the people, and the head of the state is elected[span_27](end_span).
    • [span_28](start_span)
  • Justice, Liberty, and Equality: It aims to secure social, economic, and political justice; liberty of thought and expression; and equality of status and opportunity for all citizens[span_28](end_span).
