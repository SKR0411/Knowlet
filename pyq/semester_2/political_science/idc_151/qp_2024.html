38. Discuss the Fundamental Rights of Indian citizens. [8 Marks]

[span_43](start_span)

Fundamental Rights are enshrined in Part III of the Constitution (Articles 12 to 35)[span_43](end_span). [span_44](start_span)They are essential for the physical, mental, and moral development of individuals[span_44](end_span).

    [span_45](start_span)
  • Right to Equality (Art. 14-18): Ensures equality before the law and prohibits discrimination[span_45](end_span).
    • [span_46](start_span)
  • Right to Freedom (Art. 19-22): Includes freedom of speech, assembly, and movement[span_46](end_span).
    • [span_47](start_span)
  • Right against Exploitation (Art. 23-24): Prohibits human trafficking and forced labor[span_47](end_span).
    • [span_48](start_span)
  • Right to Freedom of Religion (Art. 25-28): Guarantees the right to practice and propagate any religion[span_48](end_span).
    • [span_49](start_span)
  • Cultural and Educational Rights (Art. 29-30): Protects the interests of minorities[span_49](end_span).
    • [span_50](start_span)
  • Right to Constitutional Remedies (Art. 32): Allows citizens to move the Supreme Court for the enforcement of these rights[span_50](end_span).

39. What are the Directive Principles of State Policy? Are they justiciable? [6+2=8 Marks]

[span_51](start_span)

Definition: DPSP are guidelines or principles given to the federal and state governments of India, to be kept in mind while framing laws and policies (Articles 36 to 51)[span_51](end_span). [span_52](start_span)They aim at creating social and economic democracy[span_52](end_span).

[span_53](start_span)

Justiciability: No, they are non-justiciable[span_53](end_span). [span_54](start_span)This means they cannot be enforced by any court of law[span_54](end_span).

Reasons:

    [span_55](start_span)
  • The state may lack sufficient financial resources to implement them immediately[span_55](end_span).
    • [span_56](start_span)
  • They are fundamental in the governance of the country but represent a vast socio-economic agenda that requires gradual realization[span_56](end_span).

41. Discuss the emergency powers of the President of India. [8 Marks]

[span_57](start_span)

The Constitution grants the President three types of emergency powers[span_57](end_span):

    [span_58](start_span)
  • National Emergency (Article 352): Declared on grounds of war, external aggression, or armed rebellion[span_58](end_span).
    • [span_59](start_span)
  • President's Rule (Article 356): Imposed in a state when the constitutional machinery fails[span_59](end_span).
    • [span_60](start_span)
  • Financial Emergency (Article 360): Declared if the financial stability or credit of India is threatened[span_60](end_span).

42. Discuss the powers and role of the Prime Minister of India. [8 Marks]

[span_61](start_span)

The Prime Minister is the real executive head and the leader of the nation[span_61](end_span).

    [span_62](start_span)
  • Leader of the Cabinet: They recommend ministers for appointment and preside over cabinet meetings[span_62](end_span).
    • [span_63](start_span)
  • Link between President and Cabinet: They communicate all decisions of the council of ministers to the President[span_63](end_span).
    • [span_64](start_span)
  • Leader of the House: They determine the dates of sessions and represent the government in Parliament[span_64](end_span).
    • [span_65](start_span)
  • Chief Spokesperson: Represents the country in international forums and shapes foreign policy[span_65](end_span).

45. Discuss the composition and jurisdiction of the Supreme Court of India. [8 Marks]

[span_66](start_span)

Composition: Currently, the Supreme Court consists of the Chief Justice of India and other judges (total strength determined by Parliament)[span_66](end_span). [span_67](start_span)Judges are appointed by the President[span_67](end_span).

Jurisdiction:

    [span_68](start_span)
  • Original Jurisdiction: For disputes between states or between the Union and states[span_68](end_span).
    • [span_69](start_span)
  • Appellate Jurisdiction: Hearing appeals against judgments from High Courts[span_69](end_span).
    • [span_70](start_span)
  • Advisory Jurisdiction (Art. 143): The President can seek the court's opinion on questions of law[span_70](end_span).
    • [span_71](start_span)
  • Writ Jurisdiction: To protect Fundamental Rights[span_71](end_span).
