16. Explain the major features of Indian economy at the time of independence. [10]

At the time of independence in 1947, the Indian economy was in a state of decay due to colonial exploitation. Major features included:

    [span_31](start_span)
  • Low Level of Economic Development: The growth rate of real output was less than 2% per year.[span_31](end_span)
    • [span_32](start_span)
  • Backward Agriculture Sector: Characterized by the Zamindari system, high dependency on monsoon, and low productivity.[span_32](end_span)
    • [span_33](start_span)
  • Stunted Industrial Sector: There were very few basic and heavy industries; most were consumer-oriented.[span_33](end_span)
    • [span_34](start_span)
  • Poor Infrastructure: Railways, power, and communication were underdeveloped and primarily served colonial interests.[span_34](end_span)
    • [span_35](start_span)
  • Widespread Poverty and Illiteracy: A vast majority of the population lived below the subsistence level with very low literacy rates.[span_35](end_span)

17. Discuss the demographic trends and issues of India since independence. [10]

Since 1947, India has seen a massive demographic shift characterized by several trends:

    [span_36](start_span)
  • Population Explosion: Rapid decline in death rates due to better health facilities while birth rates remained high for decades.[span_36](end_span)
    • [span_37](start_span)
  • Declining Mortality: Significant improvement in life expectancy and reduction in Infant Mortality Rate (IMR).[span_37](end_span)
    • [span_38](start_span)
  • Changing Age Structure: India currently has a "young" population, providing a potential Demographic Dividend.[span_38](end_span)
    • [span_39](start_span)
  • Urbanization: A steady migration from rural areas to cities in search of better livelihoods.[span_39](end_span)
[span_40](start_span)

Major Issues: High population pressure on land, unemployment, and the challenge of providing quality education and healthcare to millions.[span_40](end_span)
