1. How is pure risk differed from speculative risk?

[span_2](start_span)

Pure risk involves only the possibility of loss or no loss (e.g., fire), whereas speculative risk involves the possibility of gain, loss, or no loss (e.g., gambling)[span_2](end_span).

2. What do perils mean in insurance?

[span_3](start_span)

[span_4](start_span)

Risk pooling is the practice of sharing the financial risks of a large group of people so that the cost of an individual's loss is spread across all members[span_4](end_span).

4. What do you mean by 'transfer of risk'?

[span_5](start_span)

Transfer of risk is the process of shifting the financial burden of a potential loss from an individual or entity to an insurance company through a policy[span_5](end_span).

5. What do you mean by the term 'reinsurance'?

[span_6](start_span)

Reinsurance is insurance for insurance companies, where one insurer transfers a portion of its risk portfolio to another company to reduce its own liability[span_6](end_span).

[span_7](start_span)

21. How are perils differed from hazards?

[span_23](start_span)

A peril is the actual cause of the loss (e.g., fire), while a hazard is a condition that increases the likelihood or severity of that loss (e.g., storing oily rags near a furnace)[span_23](end_span).

    [span_24](start_span)
