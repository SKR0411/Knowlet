UNIT-I

Question 1 (Answer any three) 1 x 3 = 3 Marks

(a) Define the concept of risk.

[span_1](start_span)
Risk is the uncertainty concerning the occurrence of a loss or an unfavorable outcome.[span_1](end_span)

(b) What is risk evaluation?

[span_2](start_span)

Risk evaluation is the process of determining the significance and priority of risks by comparing the estimated level of risk against established criteria.[span_2](end_span)

(c) Define risk pooling.

[span_3](start_span)

Risk pooling is the spreading of financial risks among a large group of people to minimize the impact on any single individual.[span_3](end_span)

(d) Define risk transfer.

[span_4](start_span)

Risk transfer is a strategy where the financial burden of a potential loss is shifted from one party to another, typically through an insurance contract.[span_4](end_span)

UNIT-II

Question 4 (Answer any three) 1 x 3 = 3 Marks

(a) Define insurance.

[span_11](start_span)
Insurance is a legal contract where one party (insurer) agrees to compensate another party (insured) for specific potential future losses in exchange for a premium.[span_11](end_span)

(b) Define principle of utmost good faith.

[span_12](start_span)

Both parties to an insurance contract must disclose all material facts honestly and completely.[span_12](end_span)

(c) What is indemnity in insurance?

[span_13](start_span)

Indemnity is a principle ensuring the insured is restored to the same financial position they were in before the loss, without making a profit.[span_13](end_span)
