Option B: Micro vs Macro and Interdependence [span_13](start_span)

Differences: Microeconomics studies individual units like firms or consumers, while Macroeconomics studies the economy as a whole (aggregates like GDP and inflation)[span_13](end_span).

[span_14](start_span)

Interdependence: Macroeconomic trends (like interest rates) affect microeconomic decisions (firm investment), and microeconomic behaviors (household savings) determine macroeconomic outcomes (total national savings)[span_14](end_span).
