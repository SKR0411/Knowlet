Question 1 [1 × 4 = 4 Marks]

[span_5](start_span)

(a) What is environment?[span_5](end_span)

[span_6](start_span)

The environment refers to the sum total of all living and non-living elements and their effects that influence human life, including natural forces and the physical surroundings[span_6](end_span).

[span_7](start_span)

(b) Who is considered to be the father of environmental ethics?[span_7](end_span)

[span_8](start_span)

Aldo Leopold is widely considered a foundational figure (father) of environmental ethics, particularly for his "Land Ethic"[span_8](end_span).

[span_9](start_span)

(c) What is air pollution?[span_9](end_span)

[span_10](start_span)

Air pollution is the presence of substances in the atmosphere that are harmful to the health of humans and other living beings, or cause damage to the climate or materials[span_10](end_span).

[span_11](start_span)

(d) What is the major objective of environmental ethics?[span_11](end_span)

[span_12](start_span)

The major objective is to define the moral relationship between human beings and the environment, ensuring the preservation of nature for its own sake and for future generations[span_12](end_span).

(e) Pollution only affects the environment, not human health. [span_13](start_span)Is it true?[span_13](end_span)

[span_14](start_span)

False. Pollution significantly affects human health, causing respiratory issues, waterborne diseases, and other long-term illnesses[span_14](end_span).

Option A

What is deep ecology? [span_36](start_span)Discuss its main principles.[span_36](end_span)

[span_37](start_span)

Definition: Deep ecology is an environmental philosophy that promotes the inherent worth of all living beings regardless of their instrumental utility to human needs[span_37](end_span).

[span_38](start_span)

Main Principles:[span_38](end_span)

    [span_39](start_span)
  • Intrinsic Value: All life has value in itself[span_39](end_span).
    • [span_40](start_span)
  • Diversity: Richness and diversity of life forms contribute to the realization of these values[span_40](end_span).
    • [span_41](start_span)
  • Human Intervention: Humans have no right to reduce this richness except to satisfy vital needs[span_41](end_span).
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • Policy Change: Significant improvement requires changes in economic, technological, and ideological structures[span_42](end_span).
