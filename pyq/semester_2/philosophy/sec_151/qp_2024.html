20. What is decision procedure?

[span_24](start_span)

A decision procedure is a mechanical method (like a truth table) that can determine in a finite number of steps whether an argument is valid or invalid, or whether a statement is a tautology[span_24](end_span).

21. What is variable?

[span_25](start_span)

A variable is a symbol (like x, y, z or p, q, r) that serves as a placeholder for any member of a specified set of values or propositions[span_25](end_span).

22. What is indirect proof?

[span_26](start_span)

An indirect proof is a method of proving validity by assuming the contradictory of the conclusion and showing that this assumption leads to a contradiction (reductio ad absurdum)[span_26](end_span).

23. What is formal proof of validity?

[span_27](start_span)

A formal proof of validity is a sequence of statements where each statement is either a premise or follows from preceding statements by a valid rule of inference, ending with the conclusion[span_27](end_span).

24. What is the meaning of 'Reductio ad Absurdum'?

[span_28](start_span)

It means "reduction to absurdity"; a mode of argumentation that proves a statement by showing that its negation leads to a logical contradiction[span_28](end_span).

25. What is existential quantifier?

[span_29](start_span)

An existential quantifier is a symbol denoted as (∃x), meaning "there exists at least one x such that..."[span_29](end_span).

26. What is working hypothesis?

[span_30](start_span)

A working hypothesis is a preliminary assumption used to guide an investigation, accepted provisionally without the expectation of it being the final truth[span_30](end_span).
