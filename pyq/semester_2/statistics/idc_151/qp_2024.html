What is an index number?

An index number is a statistical measure designed to show changes in a variable or a group of related variables with respect to time, geographical location, or other characteristics.

Question 4

[1 Mark]

Write down the formula for Fisher's index number.

P01 = √[(Σp1q0 / Σp0q0) × (Σp1q1 / Σp0q1)] × 100

Question 6

[1 Mark]

Why is Fisher's formula known as ideal formula?

It is known as the ideal formula because it satisfies both the Time Reversal Test and the Factor Reversal Test, and it uses a geometric mean of Laspeyre's and Paasche's indices.

Question 32

[2 Marks]

Explain semi-average method.

In this method, the data is divided into two equal parts. The arithmetic mean for each part is calculated and plotted against the mid-point of the time interval of that part, and a straight line is drawn through these two points to show the trend.

Question 40

[8 Marks]

Discuss the components of time series with examples.

  1. Secular Trend: Long-term tendency of data to increase or decrease (e.g., population growth over decades).
  2. Seasonal Variation: Rhythmical movements occurring within a year (e.g., increased woollen sales in winter).
  3. Cyclical Variation: Long-term oscillations around the trend with periods longer than a year (e.g., business cycles of boom and depression).
  4. Irregular Variation: Unpredictable and random fluctuations (e.g., floods, strikes).

Question 42

[8 Marks]

Describe the method of moving average for estimating trend.

This method involves calculating the arithmetic mean of a fixed number of successive items in a series and shifting the group by one period each time.

  • Merits: Simple to understand, flexible, and effective in smoothing out periodic fluctuations.
  • Demerits: Loses data at the beginning and end of the series, assumes a linear trend, and choice of the "period" is subjective.

Exam Success Toolkit

Exam Tips

  • Fisher's formula is "Ideal" because it is the geometric mean of Laspeyre's and Paasche's and passes the time and factor reversal tests.
  • In Time Series numericals, always shift the origin to the mid-year (Σx = 0) to simplify calculations for 'a' and 'b'.

Important Concepts List

  • Time Reversal Test: P01 × P10 = 1.
  • Factor Reversal Test: P01 × Q01 = Σp1q1 / Σp0q0.
  • Consumer Price Index (CPI): Used for fixing dearness allowance.
