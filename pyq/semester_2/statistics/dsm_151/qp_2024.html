[2 Marks] [span_4](start_span)

Define mode and write its formula for continuous frequency distribution.[span_4](end_span)

[span_5](start_span)

Mode is the value that occurs most frequently in a distribution[span_5](end_span).

[span_6](start_span)
Mode = l + [(f1 - f0) / (2f1 - f0 - f2)] * h[span_6](end_span)
[span_7](start_span)

Where l is the lower limit of modal class, f1 is the frequency of modal class, f0 and f2 are frequencies of preceding and succeeding classes, and h is the class width[span_7](end_span).

State the addition theorem of probability.[span_12](end_span)

[span_13](start_span)

For any two events A and B, the probability that at least one occurs is given by P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)[span_13](end_span). [span_14](start_span)If events are mutually exclusive, P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)[span_14](end_span).

Question 25 (a)

[5 Marks] [span_35](start_span)

State Bayes' Theorem and solve the Bonus Scheme manager problem.[span_35](end_span)

[span_36](start_span)

Bayes' Theorem: Provides the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions related to the event[span_36](end_span).

Given: P(X)=4/9, P(Y)=2/9, P(Z)=1/3. [span_37](start_span)P(B|X)=3/10, P(B|Y)=1/2, P(B|Z)=4/5[span_37](end_span).

[span_38](start_span)

P(X|B) = [P(X) * P(B|X)] / [P(X)P(B|X) + P(Y)P(B|Y) + P(Z)P(B|Z)][span_38](end_span).

P(X|B) = (4/9 * 3/10) / [(4/9 * 3/10) + (2/9 * 1/2) + (1/3 * 4/5)]
[span_39](start_span)
P(X|B) = (12/90) / [(12/90) + (10/90) + (24/90)] = 12 / 46 = 6/23[span_39](end_span).
