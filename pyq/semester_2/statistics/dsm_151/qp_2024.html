Question 1

[2 Marks]

Define the term 'statistics' and mention some limitations.

Statistics is the science concerned with the collection, organization, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data. Limitations: Statistics does not study individuals, it deals only with quantitative characteristics, and statistical results are true only on average.

Question 4

[2 Marks]

Define mode and write its formula for continuous frequency distribution.

Mode is the value that occurs most frequently in a distribution.

Mode = l + [(f1 - f0) / (2f1 - f0 - f2)] * h

Where l is the lower limit of modal class, f1 is the frequency of modal class, f0 and f2 are frequencies of preceding and succeeding classes, and h is the class width.

Question 5

[2 Marks]

What are measures of dispersion and their types?

Dispersion measures the extent to which data is spread out or scattered around a central value. Different measures include Range, Quartile Deviation, Mean Deviation, and Standard Deviation.

Question 13

[2 Marks]

State the addition theorem of probability.

For any two events A and B, the probability that at least one occurs is given by P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B). If events are mutually exclusive, P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B).

SECTION-B: Descriptive Answer Type

Answer any five of the following questions. (5 x 10 = 50 Marks)

Question 19 (a)

[5 Marks]

Define range, quartile deviation, mean deviation, standard deviation, and variance.

  • Range: Difference between the maximum and minimum values.
  • Quartile Deviation: Half the difference between the upper and lower quartiles.
  • Mean Deviation: Arithmetic mean of the absolute deviations from a central value.
  • Standard Deviation: Square root of the arithmetic mean of the squares of deviations from the mean.
  • Variance: The square of the standard deviation.

Question 20 (a)

[5 Marks]

What is a scatter diagram? How can you have an idea about correlation from it?

A scatter diagram is a graphical tool used to show the relationship between two variables by plotting them as points on a Cartesian plane.

  • If points follow an upward line, correlation is positive.
  • If points follow a downward line, correlation is negative.
  • If points are scattered randomly, there is no correlation.

Question 21 (a)

[5 Marks]

Obtain the equation of the line of regression of X on Y and Y on X.

The regression equations are used to estimate the value of one variable when the other is known.

  • Y on X: (Y - ȳ) = bYX(X - x̄)
  • X on Y: (X - x̄) = bXY(Y - ȳ)

Where bYX and bXY are regression coefficients calculated using the least squares method.

Question 24 (a)

[5 Marks]

Addition Theorem of Probability and Numerical.

Given: P(English) = 2/3, P(English ∩ Physics) = 14/45, P(English ∪ Physics) = 4/5.

Using Addition Theorem: P(E ∪ P) = P(E) + P(P) - P(E ∩ P).

4/5 = 2/3 + P(P) - 14/45
P(P) = 4/5 - 2/3 + 14/45 = (36 - 30 + 14) / 45 = 20/45 = 4/9.

Question 25 (a)

[5 Marks]

State Bayes' Theorem and solve the Bonus Scheme manager problem.

Bayes' Theorem: Provides the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions related to the event.

Given: P(X)=4/9, P(Y)=2/9, P(Z)=1/3. P(B|X)=3/10, P(B|Y)=1/2, P(B|Z)=4/5.

P(X|B) = [P(X) * P(B|X)] / [P(X)P(B|X) + P(Y)P(B|Y) + P(Z)P(B|Z)].

P(X|B) = (4/9 * 3/10) / [(4/9 * 3/10) + (2/9 * 1/2) + (1/3 * 4/5)]
P(X|B) = (12/90) / [(12/90) + (10/90) + (24/90)] = 12 / 46 = 6/23.

Exam Tips

  • In Regression questions, remember that the product of the two regression coefficients equals the square of the correlation coefficient (bXY * bYX = r²).
  • For Bayes' theorem, always define the events clearly before starting the calculation.

Important Formulas List

  • Standard Deviation (σ) = √[Σ(x - x̄)² / n]
  • Karl Pearson's r = Cov(X,Y) / (σX σY)
  • Coefficient of Variation = (S.D. / Mean) * 100
