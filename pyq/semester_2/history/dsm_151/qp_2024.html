16. Discuss the importance of the Shungas in the history of ancient India with special reference to the achievements of Pushyamitra Shunga. [10 Marks]

[span_38](start_span)[span_39](start_span)

Pushyamitra Shunga, a general under the Mauryas, founded the Shunga dynasty in 185 BC[span_38](end_span)[span_39](end_span). His reign is significant for several political and cultural reasons:

  • Revival of Brahmanism: His rise marked a resurgence of Brahmanical traditions after the Mauryan focus on Buddhism. [span_40](start_span)He performed two Ashvamedha (horse sacrifices) to assert his imperial sovereignty[span_40](end_span).
    • [span_41](start_span)
  • Military Defense: He successfully defended India from Indo-Greek invasions, particularly those led by Demetrius and Menander[span_41](end_span).
    • [span_42](start_span)
  • Territorial Integrity: He maintained control over a large part of the Mauryan heartland, including Magadha, Ayodhya, and Vidisha[span_42](end_span).
    • [span_43](start_span)
  • Art and Architecture: Despite being labeled as a persecutor of Buddhism by some texts, his reign saw the expansion of the Sanchi Stupa and the Bharhut railings[span_43](end_span).

17. Review the career and achievements of Gautamiputra Satakarni. [10 Marks]

[span_44](start_span)

Gautamiputra Satakarni was the 23rd ruler and the most significant king of the Satavahana dynasty[span_44](end_span).

  • Victory over Shakas: His greatest achievement was the destruction of the Kshaharata dynasty. [span_45](start_span)He defeated Nahapana and restruck Shaka coins with his own emblem[span_45](end_span).
    • [span_46](start_span)[span_47](start_span)
  • Titles: In the Nasik inscription, he is described as the 'Ekabramhana' (unique Brahmin) and the 'Destroyer of the pride of Kshatriyas'[span_46](end_span)[span_47](end_span).
    • [span_48](start_span)
  • Territorial Extent: His empire spanned across Central and South India, earning him the title 'Trisamudrapittoyavahana' (one whose horses drank water from three seas)[span_48](end_span).
    • [span_49](start_span)
  • Social Reforms: He claimed to have stopped the contamination of the four castes (Varna-sankara)[span_49](end_span).

18. Analyze the career and achievements of Kanishka. [10 Marks]

[span_50](start_span)

Kanishka was the greatest ruler of the Kushan Empire[span_50](end_span). His reign (starting c. 78 AD) is a turning point in Asian history.

    [span_51](start_span)
  • Military Conquests: He expanded the Kushan empire from Central Asia to Pataliputra in India[span_51](end_span).
    • [span_52](start_span)
  • Patronage of Buddhism: He convened the Fourth Buddhist Council in Kashmir, where Mahayana Buddhism was codified[span_52](end_span).
    • [span_53](start_span)
  • Art and Architecture: He patronized the Gandhara and Mathura schools of art[span_53](end_span). [span_54](start_span)He built a massive stupa and monastery at Purushapura (Peshawar)[span_54](end_span).
    • [span_55](start_span)
  • Silk Road: His control over Central Asian trade routes (Silk Road) brought immense prosperity to India[span_55](end_span).

20. Form an estimate of Samudragupta as a conqueror. [10 Marks]

Samudragupta, described as the 'Napoleon of India' by V.A. [span_56](start_span)Smith, was the greatest military genius of the Gupta dynasty[span_56](end_span). [span_57](start_span)Our primary source is the Allahabad Pillar Inscription (Prayag Prasasti) composed by Harisena[span_57](end_span).

    [span_58](start_span)
  • Aryavarta Conquest: He completely uprooted nine kings of North India (Aryavarta) and annexed their territories[span_58](end_span).
    • [span_59](start_span)
  • Dakshinapatha Campaign: He defeated twelve kings in South India but followed a policy of Grahana-Moksha-Anugraha (capturing, releasing, and showing favor), returning their kingdoms as vassal states[span_59](end_span).
    • [span_60](start_span)
  • Atavika and Border States: Forest tribes and border kingdoms (like Samatata, Nepal, and Kamarupa) were made to pay tribute and accept his supremacy[span_60](end_span).
    • [span_61](start_span)
  • Imperial Sovereignty: To celebrate his universal conquest (Digvijaya), he performed the Ashvamedha sacrifice and issued special gold coins[span_61](end_span).

21. Explain the factors leading to the decline of the Guptas. [10 Marks]

[span_62](start_span)

The Gupta Empire, after the peak of Skandagupta's reign, faced several internal and external pressures[span_62](end_span):

    [span_63](start_span)
  • Huna Invasions: Repeated attacks by the Hunas (Hephthalites) drained the state treasury and weakened military defenses[span_63](end_span).
    • [span_64](start_span)
  • Rise of Feudatories: Powerful provincial governors (like the Maitrakas and Maukharis) began asserting independence[span_64](end_span).
    • [span_65](start_span)
  • Economic Decline: Foreign trade with the Roman Empire declined, and the quality of gold coins deteriorated in the later Gupta period[span_65](end_span).
    • [span_66](start_span)
  • Weak Successors: The later Gupta rulers lacked the administrative and military prowess of Samudragupta or Chandragupta II[span_66](end_span).

24. Analyze the administrative system of Harshavardhana. [10 Marks]

[span_67](start_span)

Harsha's administration was a transition between Gupta centralization and early medieval feudalism[span_67](end_span).

  • Monarchy: The King was the supreme head, working tirelessly. [span_68](start_span)Harsha divided his day into three parts: one for state business and two for religious/charitable works[span_68](end_span).
    • [span_69](start_span)
  • Hierarchy: The empire was divided into Bhuktis (provinces), Vishayas (districts), and Pathakas (tehsils), with the village as the smallest unit[span_69](end_span).
    • [span_70](start_span)
  • Feudal Tendencies: Officers were often paid through land grants (Agrahara) rather than cash salaries[span_70](end_span).
  • Revenue: Tax was generally 1/6th of the produce. [span_71](start_span)Hiuen Tsang noted that taxes were light and the public was not burdened[span_71](end_span).
    • [span_72](start_span)
  • Law and Order: Punishment was severe (mutilation and death), leading to low crime rates[span_72](end_span).
