(a) Review the administration of the Satavahana dynasty.[span_9](end_span)

[span_10](start_span)

The Satavahana administration was largely based on the pattern laid down by the Mauryas, but with significant decentralization and local autonomy.[span_10](end_span)

    [span_11](start_span)
  • Monarchy: The King was the supreme head of state and often took high-sounding titles like Dakshinapathapathi.[span_11](end_span)
    • [span_12](start_span)
  • Administrative Units: The empire was divided into Aharas (provinces), which were governed by officials known as Amatyas or Mahamatras.[span_12](end_span)
    • [span_13](start_span)
  • Military Influence: Administration had a strong military character.[span_13](end_span) [span_14](start_span)Feudatories like Maharathis and Mahabhojas exercised significant power in their respective regions.[span_14](end_span)
    • [span_15](start_span)
  • Rural Administration: Villages were the basic units, managed by the Gramika.[span_15](end_span)
    • [span_16](start_span)
  • Land Grants: A unique feature was the practice of granting tax-free land to Brahmins and Buddhist monks, which led to the growth of landed intermediaries.[span_16](end_span)

[span_18](start_span)

Menander (known as Milinda in Indian tradition) was the most famous Indo-Greek ruler who ruled from Sialkot (Sakala).[span_18](end_span)

    [span_19](start_span)

    • [span_20](start_span)

    • [span_21](start_span)
  • Religion: Following his discussions with Nagasena, he embraced Buddhism and became a great patron of the faith.[span_21](end_span)
    • [span_22](start_span)
  • Coinage: He issued a large variety of bilingual coins (Greek and Kharosthi scripts), which reflect the prosperity and cultural mixing of his reign.[span_22](end_span)

(a) Discuss briefly the conquests and achievements of Chandragupta-II.[span_54](end_span)

[span_55](start_span)

Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) brought the Gupta Empire to its peak.[span_55](end_span)

    [span_56](start_span)
  • Conquest of the Shakas: His most significant military achievement was the destruction of the Western Kshatrapas (Shakas), which gave the Guptas control over the Arabian Sea ports.[span_56](end_span)
    • [span_57](start_span)

    • [span_58](start_span)
  • Navaratnas: His court was famous for the "Nine Gems" (Navaratnas), including the poet Kalidasa.[span_58](end_span)
    • [span_59](start_span)
  • Chinese Pilgrim: The Chinese traveler Faxian (Fa-Hien) visited India during his reign and described the prosperity of the empire.[span_59](end_span)
Option B
[span_60](start_span)

(b) Analyze the administrative system of the Guptas.[span_60](end_span)

[span_61](start_span)

Gupta administration was characterized by a blend of central authority and provincial autonomy.[span_61](end_span)

    [span_62](start_span)
  • The King: Held titles like Maharajadhiraja and was assisted by a council of ministers (Mantriparishad).[span_62](end_span)
    • [span_63](start_span)
  • Provincial Units: The empire was divided into Bhuktis (provinces) headed by Uparikas.[span_63](end_span)
    • [span_64](start_span)
  • District Level: Bhuktis were divided into Vishayas (districts) under Vishayapatis.[span_64](end_span)
    • [span_65](start_span)
  • Local Council: In districts, the Vishayapati was advised by a council including representatives like the Nagarasresthi (chief merchant) and Prathamakulika (chief artisan).[span_65](end_span)
    • [span_66](start_span)
  • Justice: For the first time, civil and criminal laws were clearly demarcated.[span_66](end_span)
